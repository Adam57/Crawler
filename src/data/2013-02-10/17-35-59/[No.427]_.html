Carmelo Anthony's 42 points not enough in 102-88 loss: Knicks-Clippers analysis

New York Knicks can't keep pace with Chris Paul's Clippers in 102-88 loss
View the Slideshow >>
(Gallery by Star-Ledger Wire Services)
By Tony Williams, NJ.com The Star-Ledger
on February 10, 2013 at 4:32 PM, updated February 10, 2013 at 5:01 PM
Print
View/Post Comments

Let the second-guessing commence, as the Knicks (32-17) managed to look flat again, this time at home against the Clippers (36-17) in a 102-88 loss.

Despite having a better record, the Clippers were primed for a loss as they were playing an early matinee tip-off, had travel difficulties due to the nor'easter that pounded the northeast corridor, and were playing only their fourth game with their entire roster intact. Even starting Caron Butler caught the injury bug when he was getting treatment in the locker room during halftime and didn't come out to play again.

Yet, there was the Knicks struggling with an undermanned Clippers' team that had every reason to fold. Los Angeles had no answer for Carmelo Anthony, as he had a game-high 42 points (18 in the third quarter) and singlehandedly kept his team in the game. It was Anthony's fifth 40-point scoring game of the season.

But Anthony's performance was about as good as it would get for the Knicks, as they got next to nothing from the bench (15 total points), allowed the Clippers' sixth man, Jamal Crawford, personally outscore his bench counterparts with a team-high 27 points, and allowed the Clippers to shoot a blistering 51.2 percent from the field. Paul was also brilliant as he notched 25 points, six rebounds, and seven assists, while Blake Griffin held his own on the blocks in tallying 17 points and 12 rebounds. But despite all of those shortcomings, the Knicks were still within striking distance before wilting down the stretch.

The microcosm of the Knicks' uneven afternoon happened as they were chipping away at a late deficit, cutting the margin to just six points with 2:48 remaining. Head coach Mike Woodson, however, pulled center Tyson Chandler from the game and never played him again, which ultimately doomed the home team, because coincidentally or not, the Clippers went on a 10-0 run to salt away the comeback attempt.

Chandler wasn't a dominant scorer, but his presence kept the Clippers' defense honest, especially on the pick-and-roll with point guard Raymond Felton. While Chandler only had six points (3-of-3 shooting), it was the mere threat of rolling to the rim for an easy dunk or layup that made Chris Paul and Co. hesitant to leave him open, thus allowing Felton to knock down wide-open jumpers. Felton finished with 20 points, as he was only other Knick to finish in double-digit scoring.

The Knicks have had a habit of either losing games they should win or making it harder than necessary for themselves in a tight win. And while the Clippers are anything but an easy victory these days, this was certainly a game the Knicks should've won, considering all the obstacles that presented the Clippers heading into today's game.
