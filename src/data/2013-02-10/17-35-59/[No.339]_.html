Muslim leaders react cautiously

DC | 14 hours 9 min ago
  • Share
  • Tweet
  • Printer-friendly version
  • Send by email

Kozhikiode: Leaders of  mainstream Muslim political parties and organizations in Malabar reacted cautiously to the news of the hanging of Afzal Guru, a key accused in the attack on Parliament, on Saturday.

The Indian Union Muslim League declined to make any comment, saying that the party leadership had no time to discuss the issue at the moment.

 IUML General Secretary K.P.A. Majeed told DC that he was not in a position to offer any comment just then  as the party leadership was engaged in various programmes fixed earlier. “We will offer our opinion after discussing the issue in the party”, he added. 

Noted Islamic scholar and leader of Sunni faction A.P. Kanthapuram Aboo backer, Musaliyar told DC that the Muslim community would support the measures taken by the government to put down anti-national activities in the country.

“People engaged in anti-national activities who take shelter behind religion, caste or creed, need not be condoned”, he said.

“I hope the Supreme Court has reached a decision after taking into account all aspects of justice,’’ he added.     

 P.I. Naushad, state president of the Solidarity Youth Movement, closely associated with Jamath-e-Islami, however, said that the manner in which the execution of Afzal Guru was conducted, raised certain concerns about the space for democratic discourse in the country.

 “In a democratic polity, such a decision should ha­ve been taken with much more transparency and openness”, he declared.

The mystery behind the ‘Parliament attack’ case was still a matter of debate, with leading human rights activists like Nandita Haksar and Arundathi Roy raising doubts about the arguments put forward by the prosecution, he added. 

Prominent Muslim scholar Dr Hussain Madavoor told DC that there was no special comment to make on the death sentence of Afzal Guru. “The decision was taken after a prolonged trial and I believe the Supreme Court has considered all legal aspects before coming out with the final verdict”, he added.

 “The death sentence is like any other and there is no need to comment just because Afzal Guru happened to be a Muslim”, he concluded.
