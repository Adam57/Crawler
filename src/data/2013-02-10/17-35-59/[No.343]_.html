    Why the Paterno Family's Rebuttal to the Freeh Report Will Not Restore Joe Pa's Legacy

    1 comments, 0 called-out
    Comment Now
    Follow Comments Following Comments Unfollow Comments
    Comment Now
    Follow Comments Following Comments Unfollow Comments

    Louis Freeh in July 2012 Presents His Findings Surrounding the Jerry Sandusky Sex Scandal at Penn State

    A report commissioned by Joe Paterno’s family says that the July 2012 Freeh report – authored by former FBI Director Louis Freeh – is “full of fallacies, unsupported personal opinions, false allegations and biased assertions.”

    Unfortunately for the Paterno family, their rebuttal to the Freeh report will likely do little to change the opinion of most individuals who have been subjected to the Penn State/Jerry Sandusky sex scandal story over the last 2 years.

    Why?  Objectivity and common sense.

    Mr. Freeh was hired by Penn State’s Board of Trustees in November 2011 to examine the malfeasance which transpired in the years prior, and the report was not released until the following summer.  Mr. Freeh had no prior ties to Penn State, and he had no motives to draw any specific conclusions.

    Point being, (1) the report was not rushed, (2) the report was unbiased, and (3) Mr. Freeh had no personal axe to grind with Joe Paterno.  Conversely, regardless of how well the report is written or researched, the fact remains that the rebuttal to Freeh has been commissioned by Mr. Paterno’s family and been guided by his family’s lawyers.

    The Freeh report is not perfect, and Mr. Freeh has acknowledged that informational gaps in some instances required that conclusions be drawn from the evidence available.

    These types of informational gaps are exactly what skillful defense lawyers prey upon, and clearly the Paterno family has the resources and motivation to employ such lawyers to discredit Freeh’s conclusions…some of which were based on circumstantial evidence.

    But in the public’s eyes, here’s all that matters…regardless of how many reports are written:

    - Mike McQueary, former Penn State player turned assistant coach who witnessed the Sandusky shower incident in February 2001, informed Coach Paterno and his superiors of what he witnessed;

    - It is clear from these email communications between Gary Schultz, Graham Spanier, and Tim Curley (Penn State’s VP, President, and Athletic Director at the time of these incidents) that discussions had begun regarding how to best handle Sandusky…and that Joe Paterno had been consulted and/or further appraised of the situation at some point during these discussions.

    And that’s it.  End of story.  Game over.

    The Paterno family can try to spin the Freeh report any way possible, but it doesn’t change the fact that, at the very least, Joe Paterno was aware of at least one highly questionable incident between his long-time colleague and a young boy…AND DID NOTHING TO REPORT THE INCIDENT TO LEGAL AUTHORITIES!

    Whether he (1) didn’t want the bad publicity for his program, (2) didn’t have the heart to sever ties with a long-term friend, (3) was in denial that his friend could be capable of committing such lewd acts with a minor, or (4) thought it more appropriate for his superiors to handle the matter, the reality is that NONE of these men did the right thing.  NONE of them had the gumption to bring the felon Sandusky to light.

    The Freeh report is certainly not perfect, and perhaps the NCAA rushed to judgment and levied stringent penalties because of social outrage stemming from this highly visible case.

    The Paterno family is, understandably, motivated to clear their father’s name…and maybe the Freeh rebuttal will do just that in their own minds.

    But if they believe their commissioned report is going to cause the general public to have an epiphany regarding Joe Paterno’s legacy, they are wasting time and money.

    Because common sense dictates that their father and husband, Joe Paterno, was among several individuals who could have spoken out and saved the lives of countless abused children.

    His unspoken words then will forever ring louder than any written words…especially when those words are written by lawyers commissioned by the people – his family – that stand to gain the most from restoring Joe Pa’s legacy.
