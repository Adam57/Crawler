Clippers 102, Knicks 88

Slowing Anthony, Clippers Run Away With Victory

By NATE TAYLOR
Published: February 10, 2013

In some games, Carmelo Anthony can overpower teams with his superior skills on offense. Anthony did that just two days ago against the Minnesota Timberwolves, when he scored 36 points to rescue the Knicks for a much-needed victory.

On Sunday, Anthony filled that role for three quarters against the Los Angeles Clippers at Madison Square Garden. Yet when the Knicks needed him most in the final minutes, Anthony was held down by the second-oldest player in the league, Grant Hill.

Anthony scored only 4 of his game-high 42 points in the fourth quarter, which allowed the Clippers, one of the best teams in the Western Conference, to run away with a 102-88 victory Sunday afternoon in front of a national television audience. The loss was the Knicks’ second in three games.

“We had our spurts today, but we didn’t put a complete game together,” Anthony said. “We made it a close game, and then those guys made some hustle plays, got some offensive rebounds and made some tough shots to open the game up.”Hill, 40, held Anthony to only three shot attempts in the fourth quarter by never leaving him.

“He’s a great player, and I have so much respect for him,” Hill said of Anthony, adding “He’s a challenge, but I was trying to make him work and not let him get anything easy.”

Chris Paul showed why he, along with Anthony, is a most valuable player candidate this season. Paul led the Clippers with 25 points and 7 assists. On Friday, Paul scored a season-low 3 points against the Miami Heat.

Although Paul does not have exceptional speed, the Knicks continued to have problems guarding point guards. Paul maneuvered to whatever spot he wanted on the court and continually made timely plays.

His biggest shot of the game came on driving runner that put the Clippers ahead by 94-86 with 2 minutes 24 seconds left. Paul scored 7 points in the final period and prevented the Garden crowd’s usual “M-V-P” chants for Anthony.

The Clippers (36-17) also went on a 19-5 run in the fourth quarter.

Anthony did not receive much help from his teammates. The Knicks’ bench combined to go 5 of 19 from the field for only 15 points.

“I thought we had the energy we needed,” said Tyson Chandler, who had 6 points and 11 rebounds. “We just made a couple of mistakes, and for whatever reason, we didn’t finish the game the right way.”The Clippers, who have some of the best reserves in the league, received 48 points off the bench. Jamal Crawford, a sixth man of the year candidate, finished with a team-high 27 points. Eric Bledsoe also added 13 points off the bench.“This team is very talented,” Coach Mike Woodson said of the Clippers before the game. “They have offensive weapons all over the floor. I don’t think you can just key in on one guy.”

The Knicks spent much of the first half trailing the Clippers, who led by as many as 13 points. Carmelo Anthony had 20 points in the first half, but the rest of the Knicks struggled on both ends of the court.

The Knicks (32-17) started the second half on a 14-6 run to tie the score at 58-58 with 6 minutes 9 seconds remaining. Anthony tied the score with a 3-pointer, then swished another 3 in transition to give the Knicks their first lead of the half, 61-60. He finished the third quarter with 18 points.

Raymond Felton (25 points) left the game with 3:26 remaining in the third quarter with what appeared to be a neck injury. Felton made a driving layup and his head landed on Amar’e Stoudemire’s arm. Then Felton’s head appeared to snap back when he bumped Lamar Odom. Felton lay motionless on the floor for about a minute before he was helped to the Knicks’ locker room. Felton returned to the court for the start of the fourth quarter. On the Knicks’ first possession of the quarter, Felton found Amar’e Stoudemire for dunk on a pick-and-roll play that resulted in a 3-point play.

Still, the Knicks desperately needed Anthony down the stretch. This time, though, he was unable to produced thrilling highlights. It was Paul who quieted the crowd and left the Garden with a better overall performance.

REBOUNDS

Before the game, Mike Woodson denied a report that Iman Shumpert could be traded to the Phoenix Suns before the Feb. 21 trade deadline. The report Saturday said the Suns wanted to acquire Shumpert, a second-year player, for Jared Dudley and a possible future first-round pick. Shumpert entered Sunday averaging 5.6 points and 3.3 rebounds in 11 games this season after returning from knee surgery. “I don’t think you ever tell a player he’s not going anywhere,” Woodson said of Shumpert. “That’s kind of out of my hands. If the owner came to me and said, ‘Hey, we have to do a deal,’ then you do the deal. I sat him down, and I told him, and he knows how I feel about him.” Woodson said his daughter Alexis, who along with Shumpert went to Georgia Tech, asked him about the rumor. “I told her, ‘Iman’s going to be right here with me,” he said. Shumpert had 2 points in 21 minutes Sunday.
