By Robert Schroeder, MarketWatch

WASHINGTON (MarketWatch) — Sen. Lindsey Graham said Sunday he plans to delay the confirmations of President Barack Obama’s nominees to head the CIA and the Pentagon until he gets more information about the Sept. 11 attack on the U.S. consulate in Benghazi, Libya.

Graham, a South Carolina Republican, said he will put a “hold” on the nominations of former Sen. Chuck Hagel to be defense secretary and Obama counterterrorism adviser John Brennan to head the Central Intelligence Agency until the White House provides more answers about the attack and its response.

Republicans have criticized the Obama administration for its handling of the attack, in which four Americans were killed.

Any of the 100 senators can put a “hold” on a presidential nomination to delay consideration of a presidential nomination and a confirmation vote.

“No confirmation without information,” Graham said Sunday on CBS’s “Face the Nation.”


Reuters
Enlarge Image
Former U.S. Sen. Chuck Hagel, Pesident Obama’s nominee to be secretary of defense.

Hagel, a former Republican senator from Nebraska, is an outspoken opponent of the $85 billion in budget cuts known as sequestration that are set to begin on March 1.

Designed to be split between military and domestic programs, they have drawn fire from the aerospace and other industries. While informal, the hold may indicate that Graham will formally oppose the nominations from coming to the floor by using a filibuster.

Sen. John McCain, an Arizona Republican who also has criticized Hagel, said Sunday that he didn’t think senators should filibuster his nomination.
