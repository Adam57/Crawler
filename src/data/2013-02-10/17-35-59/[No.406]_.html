    Snow Can't Stop 'Identity Thief' From Stealing Huge Box Office

    'Identity Thief' ruled at the box office this weekend.

    I guess movie-goers were feeling starved for a comedy. The Jason Bateman – Melissa McCarthy comedy Identity Thief topped the box office this weekend with an estimated $36.6 million, according to Exhibitor Relations. That’s despite a massive snow storm that kept many folks on the Eastern Seaboard at home.

    It’s only the second weekend in February but to put Identity Thief‘s opening in perspective, consider the fact that it’s the biggest opening of the year so far. The film earned more than Bridesmaids did when it opened in May 2011 and that film went on to earn $$288 million globally. Identity Thief has an estimated budget of $35 million. It covered that this weekend (although half of all receipts go back to theater owners).

    So while Identity Thief is sure to turn a very healthy profit for Universal, it probably isn’t the next Bridesmaids. Bridesmaids was an incredibly resilient movie. Most films drop at least by 50% their second weekend out. Bridesmaids‘ box office only fell 20%. You can check out this chart at Box Office Mojo to see how slowly the box office tapered off for the film. That means Bridesmaids had great word of mouth. It also had good reviews from critics. The film earned 90 out of 100 on Rotten Tomatoes.

    Identity Thief hasn’t done as well with the critics. The film gets a terrible 24 out of 100 on Rotten Tomatoes. And movie-goers aren’t raving about it. The film gets a very middle-of-the-road B rating from audiences, according to Cinemascore.

    The fall out from Identity Thief’s good opening is that the middle of February will now be viewed as a good place to open comedies. Action films like Contraband and The Grey had been viewed as safe winter bets but Mark Wahlberg‘s Broken City flopped badly last month. Expect many studios to now switch their winter focus to comedies.

    Top Five Movies (Via Exhibitor Relations)

    1. Identity Thief — $36.6 million
    2. Warm Bodies — $11.5 million
    3. Side Effects — $10 million
    4. Silver Linings Playbook — $6.9 million
    5. Hansel & Gretel: Witch Hunters — $5.7 million
