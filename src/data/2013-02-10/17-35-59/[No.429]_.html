Live blog recap: Detroit Red Wings run winning streak to three after crazy finish vs. Kings

By Brendan Savage | bsavage@mlive.com MLive.com
on February 10, 2013 at 11:30 AM, updated February 10, 2013 at 3:23 PM
Print
View/Post Comments
Detroit Red Wings captain Henrik Zetterberg attempts to keep the puck from Edmonton's Ryan Nugent-Hopkins as teammate Niklas Kronwall looks on in during Saturday's 2-1 victory. AP Photo

(If you're viewing this on MLive's mobile app, click here for the full version, where you can get regular updates and comment.)

THIRD PERIOD

Red Wings 3, Kings 2

3:02 p.m. -- Ericsson puts the Red Wings back on top with 4.5 seconds left. Crazy final minute. Puck trickles across goal line.

2:59 p.m. -- Kings pull the goalie and score less than 10 seconds later. Martinez with 52.8 seconds left.

2:55 p.m. -- Red Wings back on the power play as Brown goes off for tripping with 4:09 left.

2:51 p.m. -- Red Wings have to be getting gassed after playing yesterday afternoon but they're hanging on with 5:50 left. Third straight game they've allowed just one goal ... so far.

2:44 p.m. -- Red Wings on the power play as Martinez goes off for high sticking at 10:14.

2:42 p.m. -- Red Wings have come back to life a bit offensively. They're outshooting the Kings 6-3 in this period with 11:00 left.

2:27 p.m. -- Los Angeles win the faceoff and the Red Wings-Kings are underway in the final period.

SECOND INTERMISSION

2:24 p.m. -- Quick stats: Kings have 21 scoring chances to Red Wings eight, have won 27 faceoffs to Detroit's 17. Both teams have 17 hits. Kings 0-for-3 on the power play, Red Wings 1-for-2.

SECOND PERIOD

Red Wings 2, Kings 1

2:09 p.m. -- End of two. Red Wings outshot 23-9 in the period, getting outshot 38-20 for the game. Without Jimmy Howard they'd be in a huge hole.

1:58 p.m. -- Kings controlling the play; shots on goal are 19-5 L.A. this period and 34-16 for the game. Howard is the difference.

1:52 p.m. -- Red Wings getting outshot 13-5 this period.

1:49 p.m. -- Kings on the power play as Cleary goes off for hooking at 9:47.

1:41 p.m. -- Jimmy Howard has been nothing short of outstanding for the Red Wings, who are getting outshot 7-4 this period and 22-15 for the game with 13:15 left.

1:33 p.m. -- Kings on the PP as Datsyuk goes off for holding at 35 seconds.

1:32 p.m. -- Red Wings-Kings underway against at Joe Louis Arena.

FIRST INTERMISSION

1:23 p.m. -- Some quick stats: Kings 0-for-1 on PP, Red Wings 1-for-2; Red Wings have 12 hits to Kings' nine, eight giveaways to LA's two and two takeaways to Kings' one. Kings have three blocked shots to Wings' two and both teams have won 11 faceoffs.

FIRST PERIOD

Red Wings 2, Kings 1

1:13 p.m. -- End of one. Kings outshoot Red Wings 15-11.

1:11 p.m. -- Goals in the first or last minute of a period are never good and the Red Wings just gave one up. Kopitar puts back a rebound with 40.3 seconds left.

1:08 p.m. -- Red Wings strike again after a line change, Zetterberg to Brunner, who scores from the right faceoff circle for his team-leading sixth of the season at 17:28.

1:03 p.m. -- Red Wings have really picked up the pace since the Datsyuk goal. Shots on goal: Detroit 10-8.

12:52 p.m. -- Red Wings back on power play for second time as Kopitar goes off for tripping Cleary at 8:17.

12:45 p.m. -- Datsyuk takes a pass from Zetterberg, slips between four defenders and buries it under the crossbar at 5:44 on the power play.

12:43 p.m. -- Red Wings get a 3-on-1 during penalty kill. Kings penalty to Justin Williams for interference at 4:44. Red Wings will have PP for 1:33 when Kronwall penalty expires.

12:41 p.m. -- Kings have lots of jump early; Red Wings do not. Maybe still feeling yesterday's game? Los Angeles gets first power play when Kronwall goes off for high sticking at 3:11.

12:36 p.m. -- Los Angeles wins the faceoff and the Red Wings-Kings are underway. 

PREGAME

DETROIT – The Detroit Red Wings will be attempting to win three straight games for the first time this season when they take the ice this afternoon at Joe Louis Arena.

But to do it, they're going to have to beat the reigning Stanley Cup champions.

The Los Angeles Kings (3-4-2) will be in town for the second of Detroit's four straight games at Joe Louis Arena. That's the longest home stand of the season for the Red Wings (6-4-1).

The game will mark the Red Wings' first appearance of the season on national television as it will be televised by NBC Sports.

Faceoff is scheduled for 12:30 p.m.

The Red Wings are coming off victories over St. Louis (5-1) Thursday on the road and Saturday at home against Edmonton (2-1).

Los Angeles has lost three of its last four.
