DORNER MANHUNT: Search renewed; $1 million reward offered

As the hunt for Christopher Dorner, the rage-filled former LAPD officer suspected of gunning down three people continued in icy Big Bear on Sunday, Feb. 10, the Los Angeles Police Department announced a $1 million reward for his capture.

A 1 p.m. news conference in Los Angeles was attended by Riverside Mayor William “Rusty” Bailey and Riverside Police Chief Sergio Diaz. During the event, Los Angeles Mayor Antonio Villlaraigosa, said a $1 million reward was being offered.

The manhunt has been scaled back in the mountaintop community of Big Bear Lake where about 25 deputies and a helicopter crew are continuing to check unoccupied homes and other potential hiding places.

After three days of intense door-to-door searching by hundreds of officers, the number of unchecked cabins, rentals and vacation homes has dwindled, say San Bernardino County sheriff's officials.

And there have been no reported sightings of Dorner in the vicinity and new evidence found there, investigators said Sunday, Feb. 10.


Police were seen packing up equipment, including a white board, and had vacated the parking lot at Bear Mountain Resort where vehicles had set up. Also, local officers were performing routine tasks such as using radar guns to check for speeders.
 

 At Big Bear Community Church, Pastor Don Foor said that if he was going to mention Dorner to his 300 congregants Sunday, it would be only in passing. Foor said he did not have a nervous flock that needed comforting, and that making Dorner leaving and possibly torching his vehicle in town a point of his sermon might only panic his congregation.
 

"Nothing (violent) happened," Foor said. "If something had happened, there would be fear, there would be something to rally people around. Biblically, fear is a snare, fear is a trap."
 

 Foor said he did not have a spiritual viewpoint of Dorner. Foor said he sees Dorner simply as someone who went bad.

Dorner is suspected of slaying a couple in Irvine where the dead woman was identified as Monica Quan, the daughter of a retired LAPD captain who represented Doroner in the disciplinary proceedings that led to his 2009 firing for making false accusations of police brutality against his training officer.

On early Thursday, Dorner allegedly shot and grazed an LAPD officer in Corona, then shot two Riverside police officers, killing one and wounding the other.

Riverside police said they believe Dorner killed a 34-year-old field-training officer and wounded a 27-year-old officer who was driving their patrol car when they were attacked about 1:35 a.m. Thursday while stopped at a red light at the corner of Magnolia and Arlington avenues.

Authorities have not been hurting for tips from the public.

Around 12:30 a.m. Sunday, a man matching Dorner's physical characteristics was spotted walking west on Magnolia Avenue near Bandini Avenue, on the north side of the street, not far from the shooting site. A motorcycle officer and two units raced down Magnolia and stopped him. After stopping for a few minutes, police left the area.

Saturday, a California Highway Patrol car pulled alongside a black Mercedes with tinted windows on Highway 91 in Yorba Linda. The officer ordered the driver to lower his window. Apparently satisfied that the driver was not Dorner, the officer sped off.

Friday night, Dorner was reported seen in Perris and the desert town of Needles -- 10 minutes apart.

Dorner, 33, is described as African-American, 6 feet and 270 pounds with short hair.

Dorner was fired from the LAPD in 2009 after he was found to have falsely accused a sergeant of police brutality. He vowed to wage war on the department and LAPD officers’ families in a lengthy online manifesto.

Authorities say Dorner’s rampage began Feb. 3, when he shot and killed Cal State Fullerton assistant women’s basketball coach Monica Quan and her fiancé, Keith Lawrence, in a parking structure outside their Irvine condominium.

Quan is the daughter of a retired LAPD captain who represented Dorner during his disciplinary proceedings.

Dorner is accused of trying unsuccessfully to steal a boat Wednesday night from a San Diego-area yacht club to flee to Mexico. His wallet and identification were later found near San Diego’s airport.

Authorities said Dorner was spotted at a Corona gas station after 1 a.m. Thursday. A man flagged down LAPD officers sent to Corona to protect someone on Dorner’s manifesto.

The officers pursued the suspect vehicle on Interstate 15 to Magnolia Avenue in Corona, where authorities said Dorner fired at the officers with a rifle. One officer suffered a graze wound to his head.

Around 1:35 a.m., two Riverside police officers on routine patrol were shot repeatedly at Arlington and Magnolia avenues. One officer died and the other is in intensive care.

Late that morning, Dorner’s grey Nissan Titan pickup was found burning in Big Bear.

Authorities have also searched Dorner’s home in Las Vegas. Unconfirmed sightings also put him at the U.S.-Mexico border, according to broadcast reports.
