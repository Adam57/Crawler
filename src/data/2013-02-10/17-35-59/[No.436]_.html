No. 10 Ohio State loses at home to No. 1 Indiana, as three Hoosiers score 20 points or more

christian-watford-deshaun-thomas-ohio-state-indiana-basketball
Indiana's Christian Watford was one of three Hoosiers to score 20 points against the Buckeyes on Sunday, while Deshaun Thomas (1) carried the scoring load for Ohio State with 26 points in Indiana's 81-68 win. (Photo by AP)
By Doug Lesmerises, The Plain Dealer The Plain Dealer
on February 10, 2013 at 4:49 PM, updated February 10, 2013 at 5:10 PM
Print
View/Post Comments


COLUMBUS – No 1. Indiana beat No. 10 Ohio State today, 81-68, while controlling the game from beginning to end at Value City Arena.

Indiana's top three players – Victor Oladipo (26 points), Cody Zeller (24 points) and Christian Watford (20 points) - combined to shoot 22 of 32 from the field (69 percent) and the Buckeyes' typically tight defense didn't have a way to stop any of them.

The Hoosiers (21-3, 9-2) may have lost their top ranking because of Wednesday's loss at Illinois, but the Hoosiers sure played like the best team in the country, and they stayed tied for first place in the Big Ten with Michigan State. Michigan and Wisconsin are a game back at 8-3, while the Buckeyes (17-6, 7-4) are now two games out of first in fifth place.

Victor Oladipo scored a career-high 26 points against Ohio State on Sunday. AP  

Ohio State is also now 1-6 against ranked teams this season.

Deshaun Thomas led the Buckeyes with 26 points on 8-of-20 shooting, while Aaron Craft added 16 points and LaQuinton Ross had 11. While Indiana shot 53 percent from the field, the Buckeyes shot 43 percent.

After a week that included a two-point overtime road loss at No. 3 Michigan before falling to No. 1, it has to get easier from here for the Buckeyes, who next week host Northwestern on Thursday, then visit Wisconsin on Sunday.

"The league is ... wow," Ohio State coach Thad Matta said.

Ohio State's streak of 121 games without losing consecutive contests, the longest in the country, also ended Sunday.
