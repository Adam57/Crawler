Jimmy Howard leads Red Wings past Kings

Kevin Allen, USA TODAY Sports5:20p.m. EST February 10, 2013

Detroit Red Wings goalie Jimmy Howard makes a save as Los Angeles Kings center Mike Richards looks for a rebound in the first period Sunday afternoon.(Photo: Rick Osentoski, USA TODAY Sports)

Story Highlights

  • Detroit goalie Jimmy Howard makes 45 saves to beat Kings
  • Jonathan Ericsson scores with 4.5 seconds left after Kings' late tying goal
  • Damien Brunner and Pavel Datsyuk also score for Red Wings

DETROIT â€“ The Detroit Red Wings used to be described as highly skilled and now the more apt description may be iron-willed.

With all-time great Nick Lidstrom retiring last summer and Brad Stuart deciding to move San Jose, the Red Wings were viewed as a team in transition. When they lost two more defensemen (Brendan Smith and Carlo Colaiacovo) to injury, they were seen as a team in trouble.

But Sunday afternoon, on a nationally televised game by NBC, the Red Wings benefited from sensational goaltending by Jimmy Howard and a goal by Jonathan Ericsson to beat the defending Stanley Cup champion Los Angeles Kings 3-2 for their third win in four days.

"Every single night points are at a premium," Howard said. "You have to get points to be able to stay in the mix and stay in the playoff hunt. Every single night we're out there, that's all we're looking for is to get two points."

Today the Red Wings sit with a 7-4-1 record. If this were a regular 82-game season, that pace would be good enough to earn them their 13th consecutive 100-point season.

SUMMARY: Red Wings 3, Kings 2

Detroit is performing well for a team that most experts had cast as a team that was now on a downward slope.

Coach Mike Babcock seemed more impressed the Red Wings survival skills than he was with any other aspect in the game. The Kings badly outplayed Detroit.

"I thought we were real competitive in the third period," Babcock said. "That was will stuff. That had nothing to do with skill."

The Red Wings gave up a tying goal to Alec Martinez with 53 seconds left in regulation.

"We have to get better and we will continue to get better," Babcock said. "We are a work in progress."

The Red Wings clearly are trying to change on the fly, but Pavel Datsyuk and Henrik Zetterberg are still the primary reason why they remain highly competitive. Zetterberg is tied for second in the scoring race with 18 points. Datsyuk, who scored a highlight-reel goal on Sunday. has 13 points in 12 games.

"When you watch Pavel Datsyuk play, you talk about how skilled he is," Babcock said. "But when I watch him play, all I think about is how much will he has."

Babcock says Zetterberg also never stops competing. "Guys who are the best are ultra-competitive," Babcock said. "They bring it every single day. Do they have it every day? No. But they bring it every day."

The Red Wings' offseason signing of slick Swiss League offensive star Damien Brunner has added another spark to the offense. He scored his sixth goal of the season on Sunday.

Since losing 6-0 on opening night to the St. Louis Blues, the Red Wings have seemed to improve a bit every game.

"In beginning of the season, as soon as they got a power play it seemed like it went in our net," said Zetterberg, who was named captain this season.

The Red Wings gave up four goals in their three wins since last Thursday.

"Specialty teams have been a little bit better the last three games," Zetterberg said. "But still, they have been tight games. But we have won three games in a row and it feels good, now we just have to keep on (going)."
