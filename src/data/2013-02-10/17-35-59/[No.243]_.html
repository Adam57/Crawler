Charlie Sheen invites ‘killer’ gunman Christopher Dorner for a chat


By Andrei Harmsworth Sunday 10 Feb 2013 10:50 am Celeb

Charlie Sheen reckons he’s the man to stop a rampaging gunman in the US by inviting him to have an intimate tête-à-tête.

The barmy actor flirted with danger when he extended his invite to former LAPD officer Christopher Dorner, who is at the centre of a manhunt in California after allegedly killing three people including a fellow officer.

Sheen’s invite came after Dorner name dropped him in an online manifesto.

‘You mentioned me in your manifesto, so thank you for your kind words. I am urging you to call me. Let’s figure out together how to end this thing,’ said Sheen in his own video message response.

Charlie’s overture came after Dorner mentioned a string of stars in his message including US chat show host Ellen DeGeneres.

He said: ‘Charlie Sheen, you’re effin awesome. (sic)’.

Dorner has been dubbed a ‘very dangerous individual’ by US authorities, who are trying to track him down after he warned of targeting police officers to kill.

The video is the latest headline-making stunt from 47-year-old Sheen after he went into a public meltdown two years ago, coining his slogans ‘Winning’ and ‘Tigers Blood’, after being axed from hit US sitcom Two And A Half Men.

Charlie recently blamed his odd behaviour on testosterone cream.

The star told US TV host Katie Couric: ‘I think I was doing too much testosterone cream, and I think it metabolised into… a steroid. I think it was a bit of a roid rage. That’s the only thing I can point to to explain (it). There was no booze, no pain pills, seriously there was nothing, there was no street drugs.’
