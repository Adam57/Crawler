I have a long list of favorites, including a number of mixed gay/straight neighborhood bars Uptown, but for starters, here's a guide to some of the must-see New Orleans gay bars. As usual, I'll be adding to this list over time, so if you happen to check back here prior to New Orleans Gay Pride in June or the riotously fun Southern Decadence celebration over Labor Day, you'll find lots more in this online nightlife guide.

Denver's Quirky Curtis Hotel Unveils "Ellen & Portia" Valentine's Deal

Tuesday January 29, 2013

Although it's part of Hilton's ubiquitous Doubletree brand, downtown Denver's playful Curtis Hotel has long stood out for its whimsical design and pop-culture-inspiration (including floors where rooms are designed with "Big Hair", "The Dance Floor", "TV Mania", and other amusing themes).

The property is also very popular with gay and lesbian guests, and over Valentine's Day weekend (Feb. 14 through 16) this year, the Curtis is offering a fun package deal that may be of particular interest to the LGBT market: Book the "Ellen & Portia" deal, with rates starting at $174 nightly, and your room will come complete with a tray of blondies, a bottle of California red wine, a "Now, That's What I Call Dance" CD (encouraging you to hold your own in-room dance party), and - brace yourself - two blonde wigs (one long, the other short). Here's where you can book this deal online.

I'm not entirely convinced that lesbians will necessarily be any more likely to book this deal than gay men - seems like the camp value of this package could appeal equally to both demographics. For that matter, the Curtis has two other packages of V-Day that I could envision just as easily capturing the interest of the gay market. There's the "Brangelina" (comes with a DVD of Mr. & Mrs. Smith, decadent French cheese and Madeleine cookies, a bottle of French wine, and a baby doll (with diapers, of course). Or you can go old-school with the "John & Yoko" - guests who book this receive a $10 iTunes gift card, two Beatle-tini cocktails, chocolate-covered "strawberry fields forever," and a package of wasabi peas with a tag that reads "All we are saying, is give peas a chance." Ok, so these promotions fall somewhere between corny and cheesy - I give the Curtis team full credit, however, for developing deals with offbeat appeal.

Also check out a complete guide on gay-friendly hotels and B&Bs in the Mile High City, as well as the full Denver Gay Guide. And take note that Denver Gay Pride has released its dates for 2013 (June 15 and 16). This has become one of the country's larger Pride events, and it's also a beautiful time of year to be in Denver.

Vermont Ski Weeks Set for Stowe and Killington

Tuesday January 22, 2013

Just as a blast of frigid Arctic air rushes through New England this week, the Green Mountain State is readying for 29th edition of the ever popular Winter Rendezvous, aka Stowe Gay Ski Week, which commences on January 23 and lasts through the 27th. As I like to remind readers whenever I talk about gay ski and snowboard events, these gatherings are great fun if you're a fan of winter sports, but you needn't be an ardent skier or boarder to enjoy them. There are all sorts of parties going on, plus other activities beyond skiing - from snowshoeing to dog-sledding. And, of course, Stowe's cushy resorts have hot tubs, in some cases spas, and cozy bars and restaurants that perfect for apres-ski relaxation.

In addition to Winter Rendezvous at Stowe, however, Vermont has a brand-new event for GLBT ski bunnies, and it's taking place at New England's largest ski resort, Killington. The first ever Vermont Winter Gay Pride is set for the first weekend in February (Feb. 1 to 3) - here's a preview of this get-together, which is partly a fundraiser for the state's only LGBT community center, RU12? up near Burlington in the town of Winooski.

For more information on similar events held in winter wonderlands throughout North America and Europe, check out the complete Gay Ski Week Calendar for 2013.

Introducing a New Comprehensive Gay Guide to Columbus, Ohio

Friday January 11, 2013

I've written about Columbus as one of the most underrated gay cities so many times that it's probably fair to say the secret is out. Still, it's one of my favorite Midwest cities to visit, both for bar-hopping, and outstanding artisan-inspired dining, plus there are a number of great attractions, from the first-rate Franklin Park Conservatory and Botanical Garden to the world-renowned Columbus Zoo. I had the chance to revisit the city this past summer, spending a few days venturing about town, and dining at some amazing restaurants (favorites from this trip include Harvest and Pistacia Vera in German Village, Sage American Bistro in Clintonville, and - for coffee - Impero Coffee Roasters in the Short North). I also brought home an exceptional bottle of vodka from the partly gay-owned craft distillery, Middle West Spirits, which is headquartered in the Short North (you can stop in to taste the fine vodka and whiskey). I also had the chance to stay in a fantastic small (gay-owned) accommodation, German Village Guest House, which I can't recommend highly enough.

I'm already hearing plenty of buzz about Ohio hosting the Gay Games in 2014, with Cleveland doing the honors (I'm in the middle of writing a full Gay Guide to Cleveland, which I hope to have finished in a couple of weeks). If you're headed to the Gay Games or just up for a weekend adventure in the Midwest, I encourage you to spend some time in Columbus.
