New On DVD: Ross McElwee's 'Photographic Memory'

Sunday February 10, 2013

In case you missed Ross McElwee's Photographic Memory during its theatrical run, you can see it on DVD as of February 15, when it is released by First Run Features

The film is an autobiographical study in which McElwee revisits the life he lead as a young photographer in rural France, working for and mentored by the man who shot local weddings and graduations.

Photographic Memory is an introspective sentimental journey, one that is filled with retrospective wisdom and wonderment.

McElwee hopes that investigating his own youth will help him to understand his son, and to evolve a better relationship with him.

Read my full review.

First Run Releases 'Girl Model' on DVD

Sunday February 10, 2013

In Girl Model, filmmakers Ashley Sabin and David Redmon follow 13-year old Nadya Vall as she leaves her impoverished family in rural Siberia to seek fame and riches in Tokyo as a model.

The teen's been told her baby doll look is hot with agents, but she meets keen competition and finds parading semi-naked in front of callous bookers demeaning. Her self confidence crashes. She falls into debt. She doesn't know how to cope.

In capturing the painful nuances of Nadya's daily life, the documentary exposes the fraudulent hope that exploitative modeling agencies dangle before naive girls who are desperately in need of improving their circumstances. Read my full review

Awards Update: 'Sugar Man' Wins BAFTA

Sunday February 10, 2013

Searching For Sugar Man is the winner of the British Academy of Film and Television Arts (aka BAFTA) Best Documentary Award for 2013.

Congrats to Sugar Man's Director Malik Bendjalloul and Producer Simon Chinn, who are also among this year's Oscar nominees for Best Documentary.

BAFTA also anointed documentary filmmaker Bart Layton with its 2013 Outstanding Debut by a British Writer, Director or Producer Award for The Imposter, which was among the other nominees for the organization's Best Documentary Award, and was on this year's Oscars shortlist.

FYI, from 1979 to 2011, BAFTA eschewed the presentation of awards to documentaries. The organization's Best Documentary Award was reinstated in 2012, in response to popular demand -- and massive protests.

This year there are two documentaries among the organization's honorees. Way to go, BAFTA!

MoMA Documentaries Fortnight Opens With 'Pablo's Winter'

Sunday February 10, 2013

MoMA's annual Documentary Fortnight kicks into gear this year on February 15 with the US premiere of Pablo's Winter, an exquisite and deeply affecting film in which the daily life of an aging mercury mine worker in Almaden, Spain reflects current changes in the cultural and physical landscape of his hometown.

Pablo's Winter is a must see!

Produced by the Scottish Documentary Institute, this remarkable first feature from filmmaker Chico Pereira won IDFA's 2012 Student Documentary Award.

Shot in black and white, the documentary has a rare air of authenticity and a stunning luminosity that's reminiscent of Margot Benacerraf's classic Araya.

Unfortunately, Margot Benacerraf's observational masterpiece was her only film. Hopefully we will see many more documentaries from the extraordinarily talented Chico Pereira.

Pereira will be present for a post-screening Q&A at the premiere and at a second MoMA Documentary Fortnight screening on February 17.

For additional information about Documentary Fortnight 2013, and reservations, visit the MoMA Website.
