Little Miss Ann - Walk with Me

Thursday January 31, 2013

Little Miss Ann has released two albums of groovy, thoughtful, fun music for kids and their families in the past, but her third, Walk with Me, may top everything she's offered to date.

Ann Torralba's approach to music incorporates themes of empowerment, togetherness, and self-confidence draped over the top of an organic, groovy musical backdrop. Pick up a copy of Walk with Me; you and your family will dig it. You can preview the entire album over at Little Miss Ann's Bandcamp page!

Can You Tell Me How To Get To Sesame Street?

Tuesday January 29, 2013

You would be hard pressed to find anyone in the United States who wouldn't recognize the theme song to Sesame Street, or even songs like "Bein' Green" and "Rubber Duckie." The Sesame Street Book and Record, originally released in 1970, contains these and a load more musical memory ticklers and lots of amusing and cute dialogue, making the LP one of our favorite classic kids' music albums.

Of course, selected songs from the original LP can be easily found on various compilations, but there's just something about flipping through the booklet insert while you listen to the slightly scratched vinyl spinning on your record player. The Sesame Street Book and Record is a definite must-have for all families.

Classic Kids' Music: HMS Donovan

Monday January 21, 2013

Donovan's pop tunes are some of the most recognisable on the radio, but lots of fans and listeners aren't as familiar with one of the most original and quirky albums ever released by a major artist.

HMS Donovan was recorded specifically for the younger crowd, a double album full of lyrics by major poets like Lewis Carroll, Edward Lear, and W.B. Yeats. Those words were set to Donovan's own music which had a definite Sottish and English folk music bent, resulting in an unforgettable combination of imagination, wistful tunes, fantasy, and sing-alongs.
