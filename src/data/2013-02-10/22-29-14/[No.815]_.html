How Dunkin Donuts Succesfully Uses Twitter for New Product Launches

Tuesday February 5, 2013

Dunkin Donuts is executing a new product launch on Twitter for their new dark chocolate beverages and as the company says "Dunkin' Donuts is putting a decidedly "dark" spin on Twitter promotions"... haha.

The sweeps are called  "Dunkin' After Dark," and the hook or differentiator it is held entirely after dark. So ever day after 6pm from Monday Feb 4 through Friday Feb 8 DD will pose a new question wrapped around their dark chocolate beverages. So today's question is "Share the best place 2 enjoy DD Dark Hot Chocolate" and followers use the hashtag #DDDark & #entry . Followers tweet their guess before midnight for the chance to win a $50 Dunkin' Donuts mGift each night.

Dunkin' After Dark is a continuation of a flow of Twitter sweeps that keep their customers engaged. Everyone talks about "engagement"... the heavy lifting is if you want to engage your customers, you have to be "out there" frequently. No one shot successes will happen... unless you are PSY the Gangnam Style phoneme! Dunkin is out there almost every week with new product launches, the most recent was their $2.99 Bakery Sandwich value offer with the "UpgraDDe Your Sandwich" promotion. People took pictures of really sad sandwiches and sent them to DD on Twitter or Instagram for the chance to win Dunkin' Donuts mGifts.

So How Can You Use Twitter to Launch New Products?

Well you can read Not Sure How to Use Twitter for New Product Launches or see how Pretzel Crisps Uses Twitter, Pretzel Crisps( @pretzelcrisps) uses Twitter in a very interesting and effective way to execute a Social Sampling campaign.

You can also follow Dunkin Donuts to get some inspiration... I cut a video about Research By Driving Around so think of this as Research by Tweeting Around!

How to Plan Innovative and Effective Supermarket Trade Promotions

Friday February 1, 2013

Esther Psarakis, founder of Taste of Crete, was at the ShopRite Grocery Shipper Show at Raritan Center in New Jersey.

This show is for existing brands carried at ShopRite chain. Brands erect their displays at the show and ShopRite Co op Members and store managers are pitched to take these shippers into their stores supporting trade promotions. Shippers and Displays increase revenue and profits for the brands and retailers. Esther said "This is a way to expand the brand reach. We (Taste of Crete) are working on shipper designs for our Taste of Crete Wine Cookies"

Trade Promotions

In-Store Retail Marketing (trade promotion) is a necessity for most foods sold at the supermarket. Promotions stimulate Trial and Repeat Purchase. Most importantly they generate important Impulse Sales... products that are not on the grocery list yet land in your cart!

In-Store Displays

click for larger image

Shipper-Type Promotional Displays or sometimes just referred to as "Shippers" are effective ways to increase trial and repeat purchase in the grocery store. They take all types of forms ranging from corrugated cases with a cut-out's like the Pretzel Crisp display shown here to pallet program displays like the picture of Chubby Carbonated Kids Beverages. The brand says that "Now with 25% LESS SUGAR! Chubby is a 250ml carbonated beverage targeted to children 4 to 9 years of age...".

So if you are shopping with the kids and you round the bend smack in front of you is huge display of fruit colored beverages with a topper... do you think you are leaving the store without buying some Chubby?

It Takes More Than Great Taste to Sell Food in Supermarkets

Retail Food Marketing focuses on many aspects of a brand, obviously taste is one. Notice how Chubby has a sign that tells retail buyers the Suggested Retail Price (SRP), 3 for $1.00 AND the Gross Margin, 34.6%. Those are called Merchant issues and without addressing, price and gross margin, you are not getting on the shelf no matter how great your product tastes.

How Can You Increase Sales with In-Store Displays?

Pretzel Crisps VP of Marketing Perry Abbenante, for Pretzel Crisps knows retail marketing, he was the Senior Grocery Director at Whole Foods Market headquarters in Austin, told me in an article on How to Make Promotional Ideas Effective at the Supermarket. The article is a great How To that should give you inspiration to get your products flying off the shelf and on to the consumers plate.
