Tips for Buying a Phone on eBay

Sunday February 10, 2013
  Image: bevangoldswain / Fotolia  
Lots of people get fed up with the game at retail phone stores. Mobile phone life often seems weighed down by endless contracts, limited selection, high prices, and the ecological impact caused by the constant stream of new phone purchases with little used phone availability.

Enter eBay. The selection is almost endless, you can buy without a contract and take the phone to your existing carrier, you help to recycle a phone rather than create yet another one, and prices are often excellent—particularly for models one or two years old.

There are, however, caveats. You don't want to end up buying a phone that won't work with your carrier, a phone that's been stolen, or a phone that won't actually—you know—work for you. Here's what to know to ensure that you make your life better when you buy your next phone on eBay.

Help! I want to source goods through Alibaba, but I'm a bit lost!

Sunday February 3, 2013
  Image: Justin Sullivan / Getty Images  
In online retail, buying low is the holy grail of the "buy low, sell high" model. Every seller is always on the lookout for a better, cheaper source. This is why sites like Alibaba draw so much interest—there's nothing more seductive than the thought of easily buying manufacturer-direct from across the Pacific Rim, at rock bottom prices, for resale in the U.S. or other areas.

If you've tried to do this and found yourself engaged in a flurry of confusing communication or confronting what seems like a too-good-to-be-true offer, you've probably already realized that there's more to international sourcing than point-and-click. Here's a primer to help you to think about buying manufacturer-direct across borders and oceans—whether it's for you, how to begin, and what you need to know.
