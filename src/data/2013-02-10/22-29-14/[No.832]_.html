Design Icon Award for Alexa Hampton

Monday February 4, 2013

Alexa Hampton received her Design Icon award on the last day of the Las Vegas Winter Market.

She is the first woman to receive this award. Hampton's signature style of classic-meets-comfort has earned her a lot of praise and garnered coverage in publications including the Architectural Digest.

The daughter of legendary designer Mark Hampton, Alexa is the president and featured designer with Mark Hampton LLC.

In addition to being a trustee for the board of the New York School of Interior Design, she is a published author. She also contributes to the Wall Street Journal, and was one of three interior designers who launched Gilt Group's Home Design Boutique. Then there is her body of work representing a number of furniture, fabric, lighting, and rugs collections.

SAG Awards Green Room Furnished By FENDI Casa

Monday January 28, 2013

For the third year, FENDI Casa furnished the Green Room for the SAG Awards.

For the 2013 SAG Awards, FENDI Casa chose to soothe any frazzled nerves for SAG Awards presenters in the Green Room with a look that was elegant yet understated. It also held touches of luxury. For that they chose classic neutrals such as silver, ice grey, mother of pearl, champagne and ice. Textures came from rich fabrics and printed leathers, and finished with twisted silk rugs.

Fashion and furnishings have an interesting relationship. FENDI introduced its FENDI Casa Home Collection in 1989 to dress up rooms with the same fashion sensibility as FENDI furs, clothes, and accessories down to fashion details including the double 'F' logo and the unique spy bag fastener.

Rare Colonial Cabinet Up for Sale

Monday January 21, 2013

Here's what happens when you buy furniture produced by a master craftsman.

You get to enjoy it for a lifetime. Fast forward a few centuries, and a lucky descendant is finds out this piece of furniture is worth a tremendous amount of money. One such lucky descendant happened to an own a Chippendale cabinet which had been in the family since around 1770. No one realized that it was a rare piece, the work of John Townsend, a highly regarded Colonial cabinetmaker.

The four-shell kneehole Chippendale bureau table was discovered and identified last fall by Christie's John Hays, when he visited the family. It is one of only seven such pieces known to exist. The cabinet is expected to sell for between $700,000 to $900,000 at Christie's Americana sale.
