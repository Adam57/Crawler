Remembering Columbia's Crew 10 Years Later

Friday February 1, 2013

At 9:16 ET this morning, the Kennedy Space Center in Florida observed a minute of silence. At the same moment, the Johnson Space Center in Texas rang a bell seven times.

Why? In honor of the seven members of the space shuttle Columbia, which disintegrated upon re-entering Earth's atmosphere 10 years ago today. The shuttle would have landed at the Kennedy Space Center at 9:16 a.m., February 1, 2003, if a piece of insulating foam had not punctured the left wing during liftoff on January 16. The resulting hole allowed hot gases to enter the shuttle's wing, which caused the orbiter to break up and resulted in the tragic loss of the crew.

Like the destruction of the space shuttle Challenger on January 28, 1986, I vividly recall where I was the moment I learned of Columbia's loss. Even now, 10 years later, I feel sad at the memory of this terrible incident but realize my feelings are insignificant compared to the grief felt by the 12 children who lost a mother or a father that day; pale relative to the anguish experienced by the spouses, family members and friends the crew left behind.

Author Elizabeth McCracken wrote: "Grief lasts longer than sympathy, which is one of the tragedies of the grieving." Well, for what it's worth, I offer my sympathy.

Have [Your Funeral] Your Way at BK

Tuesday January 29, 2013

Do you remember the jingle Burger King (BK) used in the 1970s? The lyrics included "Hold the pickles, hold the lettuce, special orders don't upset us," and the refrain touted that BK wanted you to "Have it your way."

Well, thanks to a loving family and a creative funeral director recently, a deceased World War II vet not only had one last burger "his way" but so did the members of his funeral procession en route to a Pennsylvania cemetery.

When the funeral director working with the family of David Kime asked if the funeral service could be personalized somehow, Kime's daughter mentioned her father's love of the BK "Whopper Junior." Inspired, the funeral director arranged for the local BK to prep about 40 "beef bullets" ahead of the funeral procession's arrival. Once there, every vehicle stopped at the drive-thru window to pick up a memorial burger.

The video at this link tells the whole wonderfully wacky tale, including what Kime's daughter did with a BK burger at the actual gravesite to honor her father.

Which fast-food chain do you prefer: BK, McD or something else? And how would you have your funeral or memorial service "your way"? Please share your thoughts in the comments section below.

"Grandfamilies" on the Rise Per U.S. Census

Tuesday January 29, 2013

In 2000, the U.S. Census Bureau asked questions about "grandfamilies" -- children living with and being raised by their grandparents, great-grandparents, other relatives or even close family friends. According to this census, 42% (2.4 million) of grandparents living with their grandchildren were responsible for those kids.

I consider myself very fortunate because I knew and was close to all four of my parents' parents, but my relationship was that of grandson to grandparent. While I grieved the death of each of my grandparents over the years, I cannot imagine how it would feel to lose a mom/grandmother, or a great-grandfather whom I called "Dad."

Whether your relationship with your grandparent(s) was more traditional, like mine, or part of a grandfamily, that bond is very special. I Feel Like Tippi Hedren

Friday January 25, 2013

While looking out a window at my bird feeder today -- during the depths of a long, cold, seemingly endless Wisconsin winter -- I marveled at the number of birds snarfing up the seed I put out. Naturally, the sight of so many feathered friends recalled Hitchcock's film, The Birds, and its leading lady, Tippi Hedren.

Thinking about it, though, the unusually large number of birds doesn't surprise me. Unlike most winters, I decided this year to make mine a four-season bird feeder instead of just three. During winters past, I was simply too lazy to shovel a path across my patio in order to refill it, which made me feel guilty.

Anyway, seeing all of the birds today enjoying a free meal during this harsh winter started me thinking about other ways I can "green up" my life -- even after I die.

Do you feed the birds or other assorted outdoor critters during the winter? And do you like the idea of green burial? Please share your thoughts in the comments section below.
