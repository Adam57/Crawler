Safety Tips for Event Planners

Saturday February 9, 2013

Safety and security are always top concerns at an event, and it often falls on the shoulders of planners to have appropriate measures in place to manage risks in these categories. Even at events where these services are outsourced, the lead planner must be aware of who is covering the bases. This is because, at the end of the day, attendees are going to look to the event professional for guidance during an emergency.

Like so many other facets of event planning, half the battle is communicating safety needs and procedures to everyone involved with the event. Planners should be the "know all" source for safety and security, but the best way to keep everyone safe is to disperse information through to everyone else on the staff.

There are several strategies you can use to prevent chaos in an emergency situation. I've outlined what I feel are the most important ones in this article: 5 Essential Safety Measures for Event Planners.

Planners and Leaders

Friday February 8, 2013

Being a talented meeting planner can open the doors to a number of attractive positions in the corporate world. One example would be the position of Director, where you manage other planners and implement the strategies and procedures for an event department.

Positions like this do require several years of experience in planning events. After all, it would be difficult to answer staff questions and manage best practices without understanding them yourself. But even beyond experience, management in general requires leadership skills, and not everyone has what it takes to positively influence others.

When you think of all the things that go into planning events, and then multiply that by how many coordinators a director is responsible for, it takes a special person to keep all these details straight. I recently mapped out some of the key leadership functions in this article on How to Lead an Event Planning Department.

Keep Conference Attendees Coming Back

Tuesday January 29, 2013

Memorable - Engaging - Informative - Priceless

These are all words that event planners love to see on their post-event comment forms. They represent the pinnacle of conference compliments because each word implies the attendees walked away with valuable insights. It means the conference was not only a success this year, but that it will likely draw a good crowd for next year as well.

Even though technology has given us the means to access valuable content from the convenience of our computers and phones, there is still a market for conferences that offer unique content sources. In some ways, it is no longer about what your conference agenda provides, but rather the format you use to disperse the information. It all comes down to interactivity and finding creative ways to engage your audience.

It doesn't happen very often because very few planners are willing to risk the success of their event on a creative menu choice.
