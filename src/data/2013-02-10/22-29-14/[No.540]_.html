Sharing Your Apartment: Renters' Living Arrangement Plans for 2013

Thursday February 7, 2013

When it comes to living arrangements, how common is it to live with your immediate family, a roommate, or by yourself?

Apartments.com, in its recent renter survey, found that about half of respondents (49.6%) plan to share their apartment with a significant other and/or children.

If you're planning on living alone, you're not alone. The next largest group of respondents, 40.3%, plan to enjoy apartment living on their own, while only 10.1% indicated that they'll be living with at least one roommate.

Economy, Personal Preferences, Family Situations Top Renters' Reasons for Moving in 2013

Thursday February 7, 2013

In a recent survey, Apartments.com sought to learn the reasons behind renters' planned moves for 2013.

A plurality of respondents (31.5%) indicated they're moving in 2013 because of their economic situation, while 23.5% cited personal preferences, including a desire to live alone, in a larger apartment, or in a different neighborhood. The third most popular reason for moving is on account of a family situation, such as a change in marital status.

Survey Shows More Homeowners Attracted to Renting

Thursday February 7, 2013

Many renters need to get out of their apartment lease because they're buying a home.

But according to a recent survey by Apartments.com, the opposite situation -- homeowners deciding to become renters -- is gaining more traction, particularly in light of economic difficulties.

In fact, 35.1% of renters surveyed reported having previously owned a home, up from 33.6% in 2012. More of these previous homeowners in the new survey admitted to not being able to keep up with ownership expenses. For example, a much greater number of respondents (11.2%, compared to 5.9% in 2012) indicted they lost their home due to foreclosure or divorce.
