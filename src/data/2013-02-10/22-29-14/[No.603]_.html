New Tour of Civil War Era and WWII Sites at Brooklyn Navy Yard: Feb. 16

Sunday February 10, 2013

Even if you've been to the Brooklyn Navy Yard once, you've not seen it all. There's a new tour called the "Can Do Navy Yard" tour that takes visitors to the Navy Hospital, a working dry dock, and more. Why is it called the "Can Do" tour, you might ask? Because during WWII people dubbed this hugely busy, hugely important 24/7 workplace a "can-do" place.

This tour is new, launched in honor of Presidents Day, 2013. It's the  Can Do Navy Yard Tour. Book well in advance; they sell out.

Storm Safety Alert: Beware Falling Branches in Brooklyn. Soggy Snow's Snapping Limbs

Saturday February 9, 2013

Beware snow-laden branches! This surprising, historic storm is snapping tree limbs like fresh peas across leafy Brooklyn. I've seen two big limbs perched atop of cars already, and jumped when I heard a third tree limb"crack"  and then crash right behind me while walking down the street. Guess the weight of the snow is too much to bear.

Anyway, watch out. Falling tree branches are dangerous.  Maybe I'll wear my bike helmet out to dinner tonight.

NY Transit Museum: Free Program on Feb. 10 for Special Needs Kids & Families

Saturday February 9, 2013

Special needs kids in New York have had a rough winter, given the yellow school bus drivers strike. So it's just wonderful that the New York Transit Museum is opening its doors for a "Special Day for Special Kids"   for a mid-February expedition.   The goal of the February 1oth event to embrace, entertain offer a unique opportunity for special needs families, according to Museum officials.

Feb. 10 Program for Special Needs Families At Transit Museum

  • At 10:15 and 11:15, Norma Grant will lead a family art project using MTA maps and metrocards.
  • 10am-2pm, Paula the Signal Tower Operator, a historical Transit Museum character, will greet visitors on the Museum's platform level.
  • Noon, the M Shanghai String Band will play a concert that is sure to bring out all the dancers in the house. To help prep the children , the non profit group, Extreme Kids & Crew will create a picture-based Social Story demonstrating what to expect and how to behave.

Sunday, February 10. 10am-2pm, at NY Transit Museum. Boerum Place and Schermerhorn Street, Downtown Brooklyn, (718) 694-1823.

Calendarize This, Drivers: Alternate Side of Street Holidays, 2013

Saturday February 9, 2013

For those of us who hassle with street parking, use this handy list of holidays when the alternate side of the street rules in Brooklyn are suspended. There are more parking holidays than you'd guess! They include federal holidays, Christian holidays, Jewish holidays, Muslim holidays, and more. The next suspension of alternate side of the street holidays is the Martin Luther King Jr. holiday, next Monday.
