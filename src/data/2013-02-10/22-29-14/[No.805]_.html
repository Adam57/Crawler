Elements Of An Eco Friendly Kitchen Floor

Thursday January 31, 2013

There are a number of characteristics that can theoretically make a kitchen floor eco friendly. From manufacturing and harvesting best practices, to transport, installation, and disposal, as well as sustainability, fuel consumption and how it contributes to the indoor air quality of the kitchen, there are many factors that you have to consider.

At the same time, different companies, sometimes producing the same exact flooring materials, may have different practices, causing one to harm the environment, while the other is eco-neutral. This is often the case with harvested materials such as hardwood, bamboo and cork. Responsible companies can harvest in a way that leaves the environment unharmed, but others can over harvest, causing serious damage.

There are also different ratings that are applied to construction materials, some by government agencies, and others by independent third party testing companies. The idea is that they create a label which easily identifies some materials as being more ecologically sound than others. Energy Star ratings are probably the most widely known version of this.

In the end it's a matter of doing your research, and understanding where your flooring comes from. By paying attention and knowing what you are buying, you can do your part to help keep the planet clean and healthy.

Quality Versus Price With Kitchen Flooring

Thursday January 31, 2013

Because the kitchen is such a volatile space, the flooring that you choose has to be able to withstand the rigors of this room. That can be a limiting factor when trying to decide on which floor surface covering to choose, especially if you are trying to save money.

The problem is that in general, you get what you pay for. If you purchase low end, bargain basement flooring materials, they are going to tend to be shoddy. This is not true 100% of the time, but it is a good rule of thumb to follow when making a flooring surface decision.

There are however a variety of material choices that you can make which are relatively inexpensive in general. Choosing the right material means that you can purchase a high quality rendition of it, without having to break the bank. In my latest article I discuss a variety of kitchen flooring options which allow you to do just that.

Problems With Cleaning Kitchen Floors

Thursday January 31, 2013

The floor in your kitchen gets more punishment than almost any other surface in the home. It has to deal with a whole host of staining factors, juices, sauces, and sodas, all splattering and dripping indiscriminately upon it throughout the course of a cooking day. Because of this, the kitchen presents some unique challenges when it comes to cleaning the floor.

The type of cleaning solution that you need to use on any given stain will be based on what caused it, what type of floor you have, and how long it has had to set into the material. That makes the cleaning process rather complex, as some methods that would be very effective on one floor, might ruin another.

In my latest article I take a look at different low maintenance kitchen flooring options that are out there, and explore a variety of choices which can cut down on the hassle, and mess associated with trying to keep these surfaces clean. From vinyl to concrete, there are options out there that can make the whole process easier.
