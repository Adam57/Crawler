55th Grammys 'Best Alternative Album' Form Guide

Sunday February 10, 2013
Happy Grammy day!

And by that I really mean: the Grammys are on today, let us all share our collective commiserations. The biggest music awards show on the planet rarely gets much play 'round these parts due to the fact that, well, it is a cynical music-industry money-raiser that conflates unit-shifted with artistic merit, and hands out statues to a whole bunch of humans who made an awful lot of awful music.

The Best Alternative Album category should, in theory, be a safe refuge for fans of artistically-meaningful music. But its long and strange history, and continually seems confused about who or what is 'alternative,' with Richard Thompson, Paul McCartney, the Chemical Brothers, the Beastie Boys, Coldplay, Liy Allen, and Gnarls Barkley amongst those who've been weirdly stuck here over the years.

The 55th Grammys are no different. Whilst Björk is looking at five straight nominations in the category, and French synth-pop dreamer M83 is making an acceptable debut, here, there's also Fiona Apple, who last LP out was in the Pop Vocal category; Tom Waits (pictured!), who's bounced through Contemporary Folk and Blues categories; and, finally, Gotye, whose omnipresent pop-song "Somebody That I Used to Know" was 2012'a biggest single, bar none, thereby making him no one's idea of alternative.

Let us, then, break them all down. It's your Guide to the 55th Grammys Best Alternative Album Nominees...

New Iron and Wine Album Out in May

Wednesday February 6, 2013
Iron and Wine have announced the forthcoming release of their fifth album. It's called Ghost on Ghost, and it's out April 16 on Nonesuch in North America, and worldwide via 4AD.

The album was again produced by Brian Deck, a longtime Iron and Wine collaborateur. There's a whole pile of jazz players on the credits list, and you can hear them all over "Lovers' Revolution," the first single from the record, which borders on jam-band fusion.

The Postal Service Plot Full Reunion Tour

Wednesday February 6, 2013
The Postal Service recently announced their reunion, staged after years of resistance by Ben Gibbard so as to celebrate their 10-year anniversary, to shill reissued copies of Give Up, and to play at Coachella.

Now, they're playing a whole bunch more shows; including at Primavera Sound, and at Brooklyn's shiny new Barclay's Center mid-June, when the Nets presumably won't be in the NBA Finals.

The Postal Service live-band will include Jenny Lewis, original female foil on Give Up, as well as Mynabirds leader Laura Burhenn. The deluxe reissue edition of Give Up is out on Sub Pop on April 9, the same day that The Postal Service kick off their reunion tour.

Vampire Weekend Announce 3rd LP, Modern Vampires of the City

Wednesday February 6, 2013
Entering 2013, Vampire Weekend's third album loomed as one of 2013's Most Anticipated Albums, and now it is closer to materializing. The band have just unveiled the artwork (pictured!), track list, and —glory be!— the title. And what a title it is: Modern Vampires of the City.

Modern Vampires of the City is out May 7 on XL, and marks the follow-up to 2010's Grammy-nominated Contra.

Vampire Weekend have already lined up a bunch of shows —including at SXSW and Coachella— and are promising even more tour dates in the near future.
