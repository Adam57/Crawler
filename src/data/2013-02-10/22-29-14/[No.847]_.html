Parenting an Introvert

Thursday January 31, 2013
Many parents work hard to get their shy child to make more friends and get out and socialize more. What some of them don't realize, though, is that what they believe is shyness is actually introversion. The two are not the same.  An introvert can also be shy, but that's not what introversion is.

It's understandable that parents would want to make sure their child is comfortable in social settings and able to make friends. However, pushing introverts to be social when they want to be alone doesn't help them. They need time alone to "recharge."  If you are the parent of an introvert, exactly what should you do?  Fortunately, raising an introvert doesn't take any special skills. It mostly takes understanding, support, and acceptance.

Why? Why? Why?

Thursday January 31, 2013

Brain Teasing

Friday January 25, 2013
Few things are as appealing to a gifted child as an intellectual challenge. It's surely why so many gifted kids love puzzles. They love working out solutions. With jigsaw puzzles, kids have to match the shapes and colors to get the pieces to fit together.

Another type of puzzle, though, is the brain teaser.  There are different types of brain teasers.  Some are paper and pencil tasks. And some are held and manipulated to be solved, like Rubik's Cube. Few people these days are unfamiliar with that puzzle cube, but not many know about other manipulative brain teasers.

January 29th is National Puzzle Day. Get a couple of these brain teasers for your child to celebrate the day. Not only will your child's brain get a good workout, you just might get him away from some of his digital toys!
