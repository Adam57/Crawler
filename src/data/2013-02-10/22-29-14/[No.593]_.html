Carmen Salvino Classic: Archer vs. Gomez, Hitmen vs. Everyone

Wednesday February 6, 2013

Sunday, at 1 p.m. ET on ESPN, we'll see the Carmen Salvino Classic Finals. The telecast will include round 2 of league play, with the same format we saw two weeks ago during the PBA League debut, and will also feature the individual title match between D.J. Archer and Andres Gomez.

With only two bowlers advancing to the individual title match (historically, four or five bowlers would advance to a stepladder final), the qualifying and match-play rounds were even more important than usual. D.J. Archer led (handily) for most of the tournament, and Gomez jumped into that second spot late in an effort to repeat as champion in this event.

The Philadelphia Hitmen earned the top seed for this competition based on each team member's scores during the qualifying round of the Carmen Salvino Classic.

PBA League Stepladder

  1. Philadelphia Hitmen
  2. Motown Muscle
  3. New York City WTT KingPins
  4. Winner of the Clash Round

The Clash Round will include the five remaining teams. L.A. X will get four bowlers, the Atom Splitters will get three, the Styles two and one each from the Jack Rabbits and Strikers.

Take a look at the current PBA League standings to see how this event will affect the chase for the Elias Cup.

Celebrities, Pros and Bakers - 270 to 267?

Tuesday February 5, 2013

This past Sunday, we saw the Chris Paul PBA League All-Stars event, the fifth annual extravaganza hosted by Paul and featuring pro bowlers and other celebrities. Every year, this event is a little different from the previous year, but this time, what stood out to me was the score in the final match. 270 to 267.

It's hard enough to bowl a 267 on one's own (even for a pro). To do so in a two-man Baker-style match makes it that much harder. To do so with one member of the team being a professional comedian rather than professional bowler is even more difficult. And then to lose, as Bill O'Neill and Kevin Hart did, makes it unfair.

Despite putting up a score just 33 pins short of perfect (and with Kevin Hart throwing five strikes on the five shots he took), the duo representing the Philadelphia Hitmen fell to Silver Lake Atom Splitters franchise player Chris Barnes and owner Chris Hardwick, who shot 270.

When you think of celebrity bowling events, you probably don't tend to think of high scores or fierce matches that come down to the 10th frame, and yet that's exactly what we saw. Did you watch it? What did you think of the title match or the event as a whole?

Super Bowl Sunday is for Bowling

Wednesday January 30, 2013

Sunday, just before the Super Bowl, the PBA presents Chris Paul's PBA League All-Stars, featuring some of the top bowlers from the PBA League and most of the PBA League celebrity owners, among others. The telecast begins at 4 p.m. ET on ESPN.

These Chris Paul events have been going on for four years, and while the format varies slightly from year to year, the one constant is fun. These events are always fun to watch, and this one will possibly be the most fun yet.

If you're having a Super Bowl party, start things early and get warmed up with some PBA action.

Did You See the PBA League Debut Sunday?

Tuesday January 29, 2013

Two days ago, on ESPN, the new PBA League made its official debut with the Brooklyn Styles defeating the New York City WTT KingPins in the final match to take round one. Four more league telecasts will air over the coming weeks (rounds two through five), during which teams will accumulate points that will determine the seeds for the first ever Elias Cup championship on April 7.

So, did you watch it? What did you think?
