January 2013 Bond Market Returns

Wednesday February 6, 2013

January brought a reversal of the positive environment that characterized 2012, as U.S. Treasury yields rose and the higher-risk segments of the market generally underperformed. The yield on the benchmark 10-year Treasury note rose from 1.76% to 1.99% in the month as its price fell.

Long-term bonds lagged shorter-term issues in January - another notable reversal from last year. This trend was particularly pronounced among corporate bonds, where the Barclay Corporate Long Investment Grade Index fell 2.29%, versus a loss of 0.89% for the Barclay Corporate Investment Grade Index (which holds bonds across the full maturity spectrum).
