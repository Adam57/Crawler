Obama Gives REI Executive the Nod for Secretary of the Interior

Wednesday February 6, 2013

President Obama announced his nomination for his second term Secretary of the Interior Wednesday afternoon, White House officials said.  Sally Jewel, the chief executive for Recreational Equipment Inc. (REI) is President Obama's top choice. Now the nomination will go  to the Senate for approval.

Jewell has lead REI as it's CEO since 2005, though she has a long career in the banking industry and has worked as an engineer for the Mobil Oil Corporation. According to REI, the company increased sales by 8.4 % last year to $1.8 billion under  Jewell's leadership. Jewell has also committed REI  to enhancing the sustainability of the business.

"Through direct engagement in service projects on public lands, and grants that support organizations involved in connecting people to nature and stewardship, your co-op facilitated nearly three million hours of volunteer service in parks, recreation areas and natural spaces across the country," Jewell stated on the company's website. "With the board's support, the co-op dedicates 3% of our annual operating profits to these activities."

REI was named on FORTUNE's list of  "Best 100 Companies to Work For" for the 16th consecutive year.

History of the U.S. Wilderness Act

Sunday February 3, 2013

The Wilderness Act of 1964 will celebrate its 50th birthday on September 3, 2014. More than 300 events will take place across the country to commemorate the historic act of nature preservation and conservation.Learn more about the history of the wilderness act and what a wilderness designation means.

In honor of the 50th birthday of the Wilderness Act, About Camping wants to hear which are your favorite wilderness areas! Nominate your Favorite U.S. Wilderness Area for camping for a 2013 Readers' Choice Award. For more information on events or how you can participate in wilderness celebrations, visit Wilderness50th.org, or follow the 50th Anniversary Of The Wilderness Act on Facebook and Twitter.

Do Groundhogs Like to Camp?

Saturday February 2, 2013

The groundhog saw its shadow today in Pennsylvania, which means spring is on its way. Fair-weather campers rejoice!

Groundhog Day is celebrated each year on February 2. According to folklore, if the groundhog emerges from its burro and sees a shadow, it will retreat back into the ground and winter will continue for six more weeks. But, if it is cloudy on Groundhog Day, spring will arrive early. And this year the groundhog is ready for winter's end!

You might be wondering what groundhogs have to do with camping? Well, if you love camping in moderate weather and sunshine, then you are probably waiting for the seasons to change!
