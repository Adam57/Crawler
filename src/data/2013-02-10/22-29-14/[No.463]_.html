Efforts to Create a LGBT Museum in Washington DC

Thursday January 17, 2013

Tim Gold and his husband, Mitchell Gold, are trying to gather both artifacts and donations to establish a national LGBT museum in the Washington DC area to tell "a story that unites millions of women and men but is rarely represented in mainstream museums." Although they are still far from reaching the $50-$100 million needed to establish the museum, the Golds have already established a non-profit organization to collect donations and hired a real estate broker to search for the right property to place the museum.

If you are interested in reading more about the intended museum or would like to make a donation, please see their official site. If you would like to know more about the Golds, this Washington Post article talks about how they came up with the idea for the museum as well as why it is so important to have a museum dedicated to LGBT history.

Youngest Survivor on Schindler's List Dies

Thursday January 17, 2013

Thirteen-year-old Leon Leyson from Krakow, Poland, was the youngest of over 1,000 Holocaust survivors on Schindler's List. Oskar Schindler managed to employ and protect Leyson, Leyson's parents, and two of Leyson's siblings; however, two of Leyson's brothers died in the Holocaust.

After the war, Leyson and the remaining members of his family moved to the United States in 1949. He married, had two children, and spent 39 years as a high school teacher in Los Angeles. Leon Leyson died at age 83 on Saturday, January 12, 2013 after suffering from lymphoma.
