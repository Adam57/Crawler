Pet Dental Health Month

Saturday February 9, 2013

Image by M.H. Kramer

February is Pet Dental Health Awareness Month. Many veterinarians and dental technicians offer special pricing on dental cleanings in February, so if your pet is due it might be a good time to make an appointment! Some of the major pet stores, such as Petco, are also offering free seminars on pet dental health this month.

Equine Health Seminar

Monday February 4, 2013

Image by M.H. Kramer

The University of Tennessee's College of Veterinary Medicine is offering an educational conference for horse owners, "Management Strategies in Equine Health Care," on February 23rd. The day long event includes presentations from equine veterinarians on a variety of health topics including nutrition, parasites, first aid, viral diseases, metabolic diseases, arthritis, and pre-purchase exams.

The program costs $35 for an individual, $15 for each additional family member, and lunch is included. Tours of the newly completed equine hospital will be featured as the closing event of the day.

Animal Health IPO Impresses

Friday February 1, 2013

Image by M.H. Kramer

Zoetis, the newly launched spin-off of Pfizer's animal health division, drew quite a bit of attention today as it pulled in $2.2 billion during its initial public offering. The offering was the biggest American IPO since Facebook went public last May. Shares surged 19 percent by the end of the trading day.

The veterinary pharmaceutical sales business has been a solid performer in recent years. Some media outlets, including the New York Times, have speculated that other veterinary pharmaceutical companies (such as Merck or Sanofi-Aventis) might follow suit with similar spinoffs.

Canine Cancer Study

Sunday January 27, 2013

A new study will follow 3,000 golden retrievers for the dogs' entire lifetimes to identify risk factors for the development of canine cancer. This project will be the longest (and largest) canine study conducted to date. The study is based out of Colorado, and additional canine recruits (under two years of age) are still needed. To apply visit the Canine Lifetime Health Project web site.

Researchers and veterinarians also hope to gain insight into other diseases as an added benefit of the lifetime study.
