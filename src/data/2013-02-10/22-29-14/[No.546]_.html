How to Pronounce Laos

Thursday February 7, 2013

Photo by Greg Rodgers

For years, travelers have been debating the finer points of how to pronounce Laos.

While people who haven't visited the country tend to pronounce the 's' at the end, many travelers leave the 's' silent and use the local pronunciation that sounds like 'lao' (rhymes with cow).

Laos was split into three kingdoms, with the residents referring to themselves as 'Lao people' until the French united the three in 1893, added an 's' to make it plural, and began referring to the collective as Laos.

There isn't really a final 's' in the Lao language, so locals don't typically use the European addition. And technically the official name of the country is the Lao People's Democratic Republic, or short, Lao PDR.

I prefer the local pronunciation of 'Lao', however, I've received a few blank stares in conversation with other travelers until we sorted out that the country in question was Laos.

How do you say Laos?

Whichever you prefer, the friendly Lao people would never correct you, and you'll have a blast exploring this beautiful, mountainous part of Southeast Asia!

The Dalai Lama's 2013 Schedule

Monday February 4, 2013

Photo by Christopher Prentiss Michel

If you've ever wanted to see the 14th Dalai Lama in person, you've got a few opportunities to do so this year.

The new 2013 Dalai Lama schedule includes several stops throughout the U.S. this year. If you can't make it to McLeod Ganj, India, where His Holiness resides, you can catch him in either Oregon, Louisiana, or Kentucky.

  • See who is the Dalai Lama?

Europeans will have to head to Switzerland to see the Dalai Lama this year, and Australians will be blessed with plenty of teachings.

The Dalai Lama's U.S. Schedule:

  • Portland, Oregon: May 9, 10, 11
  • Eugene, Oregon: May 10
  • New Orleans, Louisiana: May 17 - 18
  • Louisville, Kentucky: May 19 - 21

Schedules change based on several factors, including the Dalai Lama's fragile health. Check their official website for details and how to get tickets: http://dalailama.com/teachings/schedule

The Disappearing Maldives

Wednesday January 30, 2013

Photo by Sarah Ackerman

Imagine a country occupying a blue piece of the Indian Ocean larger than the size of Maine (35,000 square miles), but with only 115 square miles of land mass spread across 26 tiny coral atolls.

Welcome to the Maldives - the lowest country on Earth.

Unlike the volcanic islands rising up throughout Asia, the land in the Maldives is actually deposited on the tops of coral reefs. Unlike Railay, Thailand, you won't find much climbing; the highest point in the Maldives is less than 8 feet above sea level!

Meeting People in Asia

Monday January 28, 2013

Photo by Greg Rodgers

Even though beaches and UNESCO sites are fun enough, it's the people you meet on a trip -- the human stories -- that really stick out in your mind once you return home.

Meeting people in Asia is extraordinarily easy. You'll meet plenty of other travelers, maybe because tourists look different and are easy to spot, as well as locals who are friendly and more than willing to show you around their culture.

So how do you start to meet local people? Breaking the ice is easy -- just say hello!
