Joel and Melissa go electric -- would you?

Friday February 8, 2013

I just got word from my friends Joel and Melissa that they bought an electric car -- a Ford Focus Electric. They are excellent electric vehicle candidates: He works from home, and her job and the kids' schools are close by. Most of their driving is short trips, and they have a second car (a Toyota Prius) for long-distance vacations. (They will eventually replace the Prius with a plug-in hybrid that can use the Focus Electric's charger.) They are willing to shoulder the extra cost of the car for the ecological benefits, although they will realize a cost savings since the Focus is replacing a gasoline car, and the fuel and maintenance costs will be significantly lower.

A lot of people object to the short range of EVs, though I have long said that the range of current EVs is more than sufficient, especially considering that they can be refueled nightly right at home. I'm eager to see how Joel and Melissa get on with their new EV.

I think a lot more people could do with an electric car as a second vehicle. Would you consider buying an EV? Cast your vote and tell us why (or why not) in the comments section. -- Aaron Gold

2013 Reader's Choice Awards: Nominations now open!

Tuesday February 5, 2013

Nominations for our 2013 Readers' Choice Awards are now open! Go here to nominate.

For those unfamiliar, the About.com Readers' Choice Awards are where YOU pick the best cars of 2013. Once again, we have five categories: Best Family Car, Best Performance Car, Best Car for Teens, Best Car Under $17,000 (I've upped the price limit by a grand since last year), and Best New or Redesigned Car of 2013.

We're on a bit of a short time frame this year -- nominations will close next Monday night at midnight EST, 9:00 pm Pacific time, so please don't wait -- nominate now. You can make up to three nominations in each category. After the 11th, I'll tally up the nominations and you (and your fellow readers) will vote on the winners. (Final voting opens on February 19th.)

A couple of notes about nominations:

  • The form will ask for a Facebook or About.com account, or you can just put in an email address. You won't recieve any spam as a result; we're simply trying to avoid stuffing of the nomination boxes.
  • The Best New or Redesigned Car of 2013 is limited to just that -- models that are new or have been significantly redesigned for the 2013 model year. You'll find most of the eligible cars in this photo gallery.

Thanks for participating -- now go forth and nominate!-- Aaron Gold

Vote: What's most important in a mid-size sedan?

Wednesday January 30, 2013

I'm just back from the 2014 Mazda6 preview drive. Mazda has asked us to refrain from posting our driving impressions until next week -- a bummer, as I'm really looking forward to telling you about this car. Still, I gotta respect Mazda for keeping the playing field even. (Early reviews tend to get more page views.)

That said, it's nice to have a few days to reflect on the car while I write up my impressions. Those of us who love cars have certain expectations from Mazda -- I'll bet you most auto writers would rather own a Mazda3 than a 2012 Honda Civic, but which car racks up more sales? The point is that what we like isn't necessarily what the public likes. So this is a good time to ask: When buying a mid-size sedan, which aspects are most important to you?
