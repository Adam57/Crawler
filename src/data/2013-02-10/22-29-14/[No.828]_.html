Keeping Narrow Leaf Sag

Saturday February 9, 2013
Narrow Leaf Sag (Sagittaria subulata), is a lovely rosette plant that is easy to care for, and quite hardy. Thriving in a variety of conditions, its compact size makes it an ideal foreground plant. If you're lucky, yours may even produce a long flower stem that grows to the surface, opening into small white flowers.

Fiddler Crabs

Wednesday February 6, 2013
If you've seen a Fiddler Crab you'll remember it. There is something mesmerizing about the way they wave one claw about as if saluting each other. Looked charming didn't they? Unfortunately odds are pretty high that they were not being kept in the proper conditions.

Fiddler Crabs require brackish water, as well as regular access to dry land. What they don't need is to be tossed in a fully filled freshwater tank. Before you buy one, be sure to learn about the proper care for your Fiddler Crab.

If you see them being improperly cared for at the pet shop, you can do something about it. This customer at a well known chain pet store was able to bring the issue of Fiddler Crab care forward to a person who was in a position to make a change in a significant way. Gold Barb

Tuesday February 5, 2013
Naturally green in color, the Gold Barb is an attractive color variation that has been captive bred since the 1960s. A hugely popular member of the Barb family, Gold Barbs are easy to care for, and not fussy about water conditions. Even better is their peaceful disposition which makes them great companions for virtually any other peaceful fish. As long as you keep then in a school, they will thrive almost anywhere. Have you kept Gold Barbs? If so, share your experiences with this popular Barb.
