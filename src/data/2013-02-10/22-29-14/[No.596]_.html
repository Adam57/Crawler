Wild Rice Bread

Tuesday January 29, 2013

I've been trying out a wide variety of different types of rice simply because I enjoy rice so much and it is a nice change from pasta dinners. While working with wild rice, I learned that wild rice is not a true rice. It is a grass. Also, wild rice needs a longer cooking time than white rice, but the end flavor is worth the wait. I've added it to my breads, making a simple wild rice bread, and have had great results. I have even used true rices in this recipe with good results, making this a very versatile recipe to keep on hand.

Wheat Grain Bread

Sunday January 13, 2013

While at my favorite store, Echo Hill, an Amish run grocery store in Fleetwood, I bought some more wheat flour, cracked wheat, and wheat germ for baking. Of course, like an eager kid, I had to use everything as soon as I got home. The result is this heavy, winter weather wheat grain bread. The bread was very good, especially when topped with peanut butter or Nutella. Very filling!

All Wheat Bread

Saturday January 12, 2013

I have always preferred light wheat breads, but some of my friends prefer all wheat (no bread flour) breads. This simple all wheat bread recipe is for them. It is a very basic recipe that can easily be altered to suit your own, personal tastes, whether you change the sweetener or you choose to add raisins or dried cranberries to the dough.

Oats and Dark Molasses Bread

Friday January 11, 2013

I have been adding molasses to my winter breads for almost 2 decades, and I am still in love with the rich, deep flavor it adds to bread. Oats and dark molasses bread recipe uses blackstrap molasses to give the bread a perfect, warmth in the winter feel. It is also a great bread to make into french toast or peanut butter sandwiches.
