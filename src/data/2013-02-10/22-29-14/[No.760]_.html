You Better Improve or Else!

Tuesday January 29, 2013

With the quality of earnings that ecommerce businesses are demonstrating, we are going to hear of an increasingly large number of failures. Not that failure is necessarily a bad thing. But defying economic commonsense can get you into trouble sooner than you think.

One area of special concern is the fact that too much money is being spent on customer acquisition. Pay per click advertising accounts for far greater a portion of expense than is healthy. May I recommend an alternative: social commerce. It's not easy, but done right it can save you tons of money. And that is just one of the ways in which you can be lean.

Emerging Trends in Ecommerce

Tuesday January 29, 2013

The ecommerce landscape is getting more and more interesting as the stakes of the game are being raised. Investor's money is pouring in with the hope that it will cause the investee ecommerce businesses to thrive.

There is some innovation that is being observed in all aspects of ecommerce. The resurgence of the click and mortar model is one such example.

But I predict that the end is near for undercapitalized ecommerce businesses: business that do not have the vision that will take them into the future, businesses that are built on flawed business models.

And when some of these ecommerce businesses come up for sale, there will be great opportunities to buy their assets and equity.

Rich Ecommerce Poor Ecommerce

Monday January 28, 2013

The more a business grows, the poorer it gets. But in its manic quest to find further growth, it sells more equity, thereby diluting its owners further. And with that funding it grows more and gets poorer!

If that sounds like circular logic, or worse, extreme stupidity, take a look at many of the venture capital backed ecommerce businesses today. It has been asked before, but I have to ask again, "Is the ecommerce business model flawed?"

Should We Refer to Tablet Commerce as Tcommerce?

Monday December 31, 2012

We have ecommerce (electronic commerce), fcommerce (facebook commerce), mcommerce (mobile commerce), and who know what other kind of commerce. I have already proposed scommerce (social commerce) in the past. Today, I am going to introduce you to yet another piece of jargon in the commerce lexicon: tcommerce. As you have probably guessed, that stands for tablet commerce. Even if you are one of those that have a low tolerance for jargon, you cannot but agree that tablet computers are going to be one of the greatest drivers of electronic commerce.
