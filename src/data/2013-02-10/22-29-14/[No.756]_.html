The Baltic Capitals: An Introduction

Sunday February 10, 2013

The Baltic capitals of Tallinn, Riga, and Vilnius are different in character and appearance, yet they're frequently visited during a single trip to the three Baltic nations. Comparing and contrasting the three cities is one of the most interesting aspects of such a journey, and each one will have its high and low points depending upon your expectations. Discover your favorite Baltic capital!

Set Your Sights on Riga's Must-See Attractions

Tuesday January 29, 2013

Riga's must-see sights represent a surprising number of architectural eras, from medieval stonework to Art Nouveau splendor. Find your favorites in this city full of surprises, legends, and beauty while taking a step into Riga's history to find out how buildings survived or were rebuilt after war and Soviet occupation.

Learn About Latvia in Riga's Museums

Monday January 28, 2013

Riga's museums make the city's and Latvia's history accessible through art, functional objects from the past, photographs, and information. See Soviet-era trains at the Railway Museum or step into an Art Nouveau-style apartment at the Art Nouveau Museum.

Be Awed by Old Town Riga

Sunday January 27, 2013

Old Town Riga is only a small part of the Latvian capital city, but it is rich with history. Famous landmarks that serve as symbols of Riga can be found here, including Riga Cathedral and the House of Blackheads.

While in Old Town Riga, shops selling traditional souvenirs and restaurants serving Latvian food are prevalent. Be sure to try the Riga Black Balsam, a liqueur famous for its deep color, unique flavor, and formidable proof.
