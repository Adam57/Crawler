Work to Stay Afloat in Graduate School?

Friday February 8, 2013

Most students work at least part-time while in college. However, employment is often controversial in many graduate programs. Should you work during grad school? Can you stay afloat without working? Note that most programs that expect students to not work offer at least some sources of funding. Before making your decision, think carefully and consider your program's culture, expectations for students, and, of course, whether you can afford not to work.

Asked to Write a Students' Third Letter of Recommendation?

Wednesday February 6, 2013

It happens pretty frequently. A student who you've had in class once or twice emails or stops by your office to ask for a recommendation letter for graduate school. "I've done research with Dr. So-and-so and have done an internship with Dr. Other but I need three recommendation letters for grad school. Can you help me out?" Finding that third letter is often really hard for students. Like most instructors you'll likely feel torn by the desire to help but the knowledge that that a recommendation letter that is simply positive without offering details or information beyond that found in the transcript is not helpful to graduate applications. So what do you do? Should you agree to write the third (likely weak) recommendation letter? As a student, should you request it?

Thinking About a Career in Writing?

Tuesday January 29, 2013

Many students who dream of becoming writers consider graduate study as a menas to a writing career. Is it? The Master of Fine Arts (MFA) in Writing offers the opportunity to hone your craft and obtain valuable feedback, but some argue that it is less valuable than it appears. What is the MFA and what are it's pros and cons? Is an MFA in writing for you?
