Best Weekend Bets

Friday February 8, 2013

Mardi Gras, Chinese New Year, and lots of Valentine events are just some of the events you'll find the next few days. So many choices! Have a safe and wonderful weekend!

Roots Revival
This cabaret chronicles the journey of African Americans from West Africa to today, with music, song, dance and spoken word. Local and Broadway talent will take the stage to perform. See it at Popejoy Friday, February 8. Doors open at 7 p.m. and the show begins at 8 p.m.

Valentine Adult Night: Love Beneath the Stars
See a special show in the planetarium and star gaze from the observatory at the New Mexico Museum of Natural History and Science. Tour the museum's exhibits and make connections via ham radio. There will be tours guided by a museum curator. Dance the night away in the atrium. There will be complementary snacks and a cash dessert bar. Cash bar for those over 21. The evening is for those 18 years and older and happens Friday, February 8, beginning at 7 p.m. at the museum.

New Mexico Shrine Circus
The three ring circus runs for three days and eight shows. All proceeds benefit Ballut Abayd Shrine Center, which gives to children who need medical care. See it at the New Mexico Expo; you have eight shows to choose from February 8 - 10. Tickets can be purchased online, or call (505) 255-5329.

Toughest Monster Truck Tour
Trucks take the stage at Santa Ana Center. See dinosaur themed truck Smashasuraus, Tailgator, Bigfoot, Equilizer, Toxic, and the transforming, fire-breathing dinosaur robot, Transurus. Watch 'em roll February 8 and 9.

Carnaval Celebration
The annual Carnaval celebration at the National Hispanic Cultural Center is a festive evening of music and dance. See the Odara Dance Ensemble and the musical group PANdemonium February 8 and 9.

Mardi Gras Celebrations
The Great Face and Body Spa will have a Mardi Gras celebration this weekend, starting with a masquerade ball on Friday, February 8 from 8 p.m. to midnight. Don't miss the voodoo prosperity ritual blessing at 10 p.m. And on Saturday, February 9, hear Saudade play their Brazilian sambas, tangos and bossas beginning at 7 p.m.

Valentine Cabaret
The New Mexico Gay Men's Chorus will sing about the many sides of love at the South Broadway Cultural Center. Hear songs such as "Breaking Up is Hard to Do" and others by soloists, duos, ensembles and the full chorus February 8 - 10.

Friends and Lovers Hot Air Balloon Rally
The first Valentine balloon event in Albuquerque was in 1976. This popular launch features up to 100 hot air balloons rising into the sky for two mornings over Valentine's Day weekend. Access to the park is from the northeast corner of Balloon Park. Free to watch/attend on February 9 and 10 at the Balloon Park, 5000 Balloon Parkway NE.

Valentine's Day Breakfast at Balloon Museum
The Balloon Museum will have a breakfast buffet in conjunction with the Friends and Lovers Balloon Rally. Tickets include the buffet, entertainment, and raffles with prizes that include hot air balloon rides. There will be a free shuttle service to Balloon Fiesta Park for the balloon rally. The breakfast is Saturday, February 9 at the Balloon Museum, from 6 a.m. to 10 a.m.

Adults Only Valentine's Day Tours
Three tours are available: "Birds and the Beasts," at the zoo; "Love Bugs" at the Botanic Gardens and "That's a Moray!" at the Aquarium. The tour is for adults only, 18 and older, on Saturday, February 9.

Old Town Sweetheart Stroll and Amor y Mas
Head to Old Town for a bit of romance that includes live entertainment and sweet shopping deals. This year, there will also be a group marriage ceremony, and you can registre for a FREE walk up wedding by A Celebration of Love Chapel. It's happening Saturday, February 9.

New Mexico Philharmonic Celebrates Valentine's Day
Ann Hampton Callaway is a singer and pianist who will bring her interpretations of romantic classics to the Popejoy stage. Roger Melone conducts the New Mexico Philharmonic. Hear selections from West Side Story, "What the World Needs Now," and other romantic classics on Saturday, February 9.

Chinese New Year
The Chinese Cultural Center will bring out the fireworks and put on lion and ribbon dances on Sunday, February 10. You'll get to see Kung Fu demonstrations as well. And Ta Lin market will have lion dances both February 9 and 10.

Cause of the Week

Cupid's Chase 5K Run
The Cupid's Chase run is a national 5K event that takes place in 24 cities, on the same day, at the same time. Proceeds benefit Community Options, which provides supports to people with disabilites. There will be giveaways and prizes, and of course, the chance to see balloons! Walkers are welcome. It happens at Balloon Fiesta Park, 5000 Balloon Fiesta Parkway in Albuquerque (where you can see balloons) and at 518 Old Santa Fe Trail in Santa Fe, February 9.

Chocolate Fantasy

Wednesday February 6, 2013

The New Mexico Museum of Natural History and Science foundation presents their Chocolate Fantasy fundraiser each year in late winter. The black tie event features a creative theme, and this year's is Once Upon a Time: An Enchanted Chocolate Fantasy. There will be chocolate sculptures and edible samples created by regional chocolatiers who will compete to win Best of Show with their unique take on the night's theme.

In addition to food and chocolate, guests will also have the chance to bid on donated items at an auction. There will be connoisseur wines, luxury vacations, jewelry and more. Proceeds from the event benefit the museum foundation.

The 21st annual Chocolate Fantasy charity event takes place Saturday, March 2, 2013 at the Tamaya Resort. Tickets are $200 per person, with $75 tax-deductible. The night runs from 7 p.m. to midnight.

Year of the Snake

Monday February 4, 2013

It's just about time for the Chinese New Year, and this year's symbol will be the skin shedding snake. The 2013 Chinese New Year begins on February 10 and ends January 30, 2014. Snake years are known as a time to reassess our lives with regard to finances, relationships and work.

Typically, Chinese families prepare for the new year up to a month in advance. They'll do this by cleaning house, getting a hair cut, going shopping, and going to the fortune teller. The housecleaning is a kind of spring cleaning, with dirt being swept out the door to ensure the new year is off to a good start.

There will be celebrations in Albuquerque and Santa Fe this coming weekend to welcome in the new year. You can find lion dances at the Chinese Cultural Center and Ta Lin Markets.

Here's to a year of good luck to everyone. Now for those traditional potstickers!

Best Weekend Bets

Friday February 1, 2013

Admission is free this weekend to the Albuquerque Museum of Art and History if you hold a Bank of America card. The first weekend of the month, Museums on Us makes admission possible.

Family Art Workshops
The Albuquerque Museum offers hands on workshops every Saturday that encourage creative thinking and explorations into art. The workshops are free with regular admission. Saturday, February 2.

Revolutions Theatre Festival: Artistic Handicraft
The Revolutions Theater Festival continues this weekend. Poland's Teatr Figur Krakow will present a show of hand theatre, where the artists use their hands, arms and a sense of humor to bring the stage to life. See it at the National Hispanic Cultural Center February 1 and 2.

Hot Rod and Custom Car Show
This is the 2013 Supernationals; see custom cars and their builders, an exotic car collection, auto artists, motorcycle builders and more. Pick up your tickets ahead of the show by stopping in a NAPA store; you'll also receive a discount. February 1 - 3 at Expo New Mexico.

Little Women
This is the last weekend to see the four spirited March sisters in this beloved tale adapted for the stage. See it at the Albuquerque Little Theatre February 1 - 3.

Cahoots
This is the last weekend to see Cahoots at the Adobe Theater. The play shows the dangers of a dinner party when someone ends up murdered. See it February 1 - 3.

Harlem Globetrotters
The Globetrotters will be at the Santa Ana Star Center for the "You Write the Rules" world tour on Sunday, February 2.

Sunday Chatter
Every week at Sunday Chatter, folks gather to hear classical music, poetry and more. Arrive at the Kosmos Gallery before 10 a.m. for the complementary espresso bar. Each program of ensemble music and poetry/spoken word begins at 10:30 a.m. The program on February 3 celebrates five years of the Sunday venue.

Super Bowl Touchdown for the Homeless Run
The Super Bowl Run begins at 11 a.m. February 3 at Valley High School, 1505 Candelaria NW. The annual event has a 10K, 5K and Kids 5K, and is a great way to raise money for the APS Title 1 Homeless Project. There will be a costume contest if you want to wear the jersey of your team of choice.

Albuquerque Youth Symphony Concert
The high school students from the Albuquerque Youth Symphony will perform at Popejoy Hall. Soloist Kayla Mathes from Bosque School will perform Elgar's cello concerto. See it Sunday, February 3 at 2 p.m.

Cause of the Week

Oxfam Hunger Banquet
The interactive event has you draw your lot to see where you sit at the banquet, just as in life some are born into prosperity or poverty. Tables are separated by low, middle and high income. The event will help you change the way you think about poverty and hunger, and help raise funds for the Roadrunner FoodBank, via donations. It happens Sunday, February 3 at 6 p.m. at St. Thomas of Canterbury Church, 425 University NE.
