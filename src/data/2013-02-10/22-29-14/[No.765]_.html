First Grade Lesson Plans

Sunday February 10, 2013

First grade is an important school year. It is all about developing and building on the skills that students learned the previous school year. It's also an important year for students to learn to become more independent. During first grade lesson plans are geared more towards academics, such as developing reading fluency, learning how to spell, and practicing math skills.

Here are a few examples of what students should know and be able to do by the end of the school year:

  • Read grade level texts with accuracy and fluency
  • Retell stories, describe characters, setting, and major events
  • Write about what they have read in various ways
  • Use punctuation marks
  • Count to 120
  • Understand place value
  • Tell and write time
  • Use addition and subtraction within 20

To view the full list visit Common Core Standards.

How to Keep Students Motivated and Engaged

Thursday February 7, 2013

Have you ever been in a meeting and then realized that you just drifted off for about 10 minutes? You didn't even realize it but you were just planning your dinner in your head, and then all of sudden a colleague calls your name and asks what you think about what they just said. Now imagine when your students are not interested in what you are teaching, they too, drift off and think about something that does interest them.

So how do we keep our students engaged and focused? As challenging as it may be, there are ways we can increase student participation. I've created a two part series that gives you tips and strategies to help motivate interaction and participation among your students. Implement these ideas in your classroom and let me know how they work for you.
