Countdown to Chinese New Year: 1 day
Some may want to celebrate the Year of the Water Snake with something alcoholic. Created by Chef Martin Yan, this Shandong Mary recipe is an Asian variation on the Bloody Mary, with chili garlic sauce and Tsingtao Lager.

Lobster Cantonese

Friday February 8, 2013

Countdown to Chinese New Year: 2 days Feel like trying out a new recipe this weekend? Lobster tails are used in this version of the popular Cantonese dish, which pairs the lobster with ground pork and an aromatic mixture of Chinese black beans and garlic. The final touch comes at the end, when beaten egg is slowly streamed in. Yvette gives this recipe for Lobster Cantonese 5 out of 5 stars and says, "It was outstanding."

Lucky Lettuce Wraps

Wednesday February 6, 2013

Countdown to Chinese New Year: 4 days: You may have tried lettuce wraps at restaurants such as the popular P.F. Chang's China Bistro chain. My recipe is a little different - I forego the rice sticks and combine stir-fried chicken, sweet red bell peppers and water chestnuts in an oyster-flavored sauce. It's a great idea for an appetizer, or you can make a meal out of it. Lettuce wraps are the perfect finger food to serve at a Chinese New Year's party, as the Cantonese word for lettuce, sang choi, sounds like "rising fortune." Readers have given this recipe 5 out of 5 stars.

Tray of Togetherness

Monday February 4, 2013

Countdown to Chinese New Year: 6 days
Along with tangerines and oranges, it is customary to prepare a tray of sweet stuff to serve to guests throughout the Chinese New Year season. The Tray of Togetherness (chuun hup) is a circular or octagonal tray filled with an assortment of candied fruit and nuts to provide a sweet beginning to the New Year. Each of the sweets has symbolic significance (for example, coconut represents togetherness). Trays of prosperity are easy to find in Asian grocery stores throughout the New Year season, or you can make up your own tray featuring an assortment of dried fruit, candy and nuts.
