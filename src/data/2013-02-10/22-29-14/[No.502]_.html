Displays of Shame Can Predict Relapse

Thursday February 7, 2013
When trying to determine if someone in recovery will relapse, looking at their body language, rather than what they say, may produce a more accurate prediction. Researchers have found that recovering alcoholics who exhibit signs of shame are more likely to relapse in the future.

They are also more likely to experience declining health now and in the future, a study has found. Read More...

Pediatricians Could Help Curtail Teen Drinking

Wednesday February 6, 2013
Pediatricians who take the time to ask their young patients about their drinking and follow up with advice to quit or cut down could significantly reduce harmful drinking levels. But many physicians fail to ask their school-aged patients about their alcohol use.

Even when alcohol consumption is discussed, some doctors still do not advise their patients about the risks of underage drinking. Read More...
