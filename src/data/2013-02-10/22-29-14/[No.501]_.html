Healing with Phyllanthus

Monday February 4, 2013

A genus of plant long used in ayurvedic medicine, Phyllanthus is touted as a natural remedy for health problems ranging from colds and urinary tract infections to diabetes and kidney disorders. Some proponents claim that plants of the Phyllanthus genus can also help ease pain and enhance liver health.

Although scientific research on the health effects of any type of plant in the Phyllanthus genus is fairly limited, there's some evidence that certain species may deliver antioxidants, lignans (a type of phytoestrogen), and tannins (a class of anti-inflammatory compounds).

Additionally, preliminary research suggests that extracts of the Phyllanthus polyphyllus, Phyllanthus emblica, and Phyllanthus indofischeri plants may help protect against liver damage. And in a small study published in BJU International, scientists discovered that treatment with Phyllanthus niruri may help inhibit the formation of kidney stones.

Cha De Bugre for Weight Loss?

Wednesday January 23, 2013

Sourced from a tree that grows in tropical forests throughout South America, cha de bugre is gaining popularity as a natural solution for weight loss. When taken in supplement form or consumed as a tea, cha de bugre is said to suppress your appetite and keep you from overeating. But while some preliminary research shows that cha de bugre may offer certain health benefits (such as prevention of cold sores, there's currently a lack of evidence supporting the claim that cha de bugre can help you lose weight.

A number of other natural remedies show promise as a means of taming your appetite and supporting your weight-loss efforts. For instance, some studies indicate that capsaicin may help curb appetite by lowering your levels of a hunger-promoting hormone. In addition, some research suggests that green tea may help protect against the breakdown of certain brain chemicals involved in regulating appetite. Larch Arabinogalactan for a Stronger Immune System?

Monday January 21, 2013

As cold and flu season continues, shoring up your immune system is essential for staving off sickness. A fiber-rich substance sourced from the wood of the larch tree, larch arabinogalactan is thought to increase your levels of probiotics (a type of beneficial bacteria found known to play a key role in immune function).

To date, research on the immune-boosting benefits of larch arabinogalactan is fairly limited. However, some preliminary studies show that larch arabinogalactan may stimulate activity in certain immune cells. What's more, there's some evidence that using larch arabinogalactan in combination with echinacea may be particularly helpful in improving immune function.

To stay healthy this winter, you can also shield your immune system by getting plenty of sleep, exercising regularly, following a balanced diet full of vitamin-rich fruits and vegetables, and upping your intake of immune-enhancing herbs like garlic and ginger.
