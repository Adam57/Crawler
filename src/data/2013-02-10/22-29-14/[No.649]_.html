The Snake Arrives!

Saturday February 9, 2013

Happy New Year! 恭喜发财！

If you were anywhere in the Mainland, you probably heard a few firecrackers go off (like thousands between just before midnight for a few minutes to a few hours!) ringing in the Year of the Snake. It's just the beginning of the New Year celebrations that will last traditionally for fifteen days until the Lantern Festival falls on the fifteenth day of the new year (February 25th in 2013).

Seeing Snakes

Friday February 8, 2013

Do you know how tempting it was to add some Ss to that title? But I resisted.

So, as the snake is coming, do you not think it appropriate to seek them out? If you're in Shanghai, you're in luck. There are several places where snakes are on special exhibit. The Science and Technology Museum has a snake exhibit running from now until March. According to review from a grade 2 kids who went (whom I personally interviewed), the exhibit of snake skeletons and other snake-related scientific items is, in a word, "awesome".

The Shanghai Ocean Aquarium also has a sea-related snake exhibit. For more info, see Kids in Shanghai for addresses and directions.

There is a "snake village" in Zhejiang Province that I've recently learned about. It's apparently where snakes are farmed for Chinese medicinal purposes. I believe I'll have to check it out early in the new year.

Get ready. The snake is coming and the dragon is on the way out.

Chinese New Year Travel Stats

Tuesday January 29, 2013

Even though I tell you travel might be difficult to book, pricey or both, it's difficult to visualize why. This CNN gallery illustrates in exquisite, gruesome detail what travel looks like over the Chinese New Year.

For example,

  • There will be 3.4 billion individual trips taken over the 40-day travel period
  • 35.5 million will travel by air
  • 225 million will travel by rail

Click here to see the CNN gallery. The pictures are enough to make you wait to get on the train until after the new year period is over!

Sheila Greenspan, Contemporary Art Educator, Back in Shanghai

Wednesday January 23, 2013

For only three short weeks Sheila Greenspan is once again returning to Shanghai and offering her popular interactive group visits looking at contemporary art. This time she will concentrate on visiting contemporary art museums especially the new exciting venues that have recently opened in Shanghai.

Planned Visits:

  • Saturday Jan 26 10 a.m to 3 p.m-Long Museum
  • Tuesday Jan 29 10 a.m to 3 p.m-Power Station of Art
  • Tuesday Feb 5 10 a.m to 3 p.m-To be confirmed
  • Thursday Feb 7 10 a.m to 3 p.m-China Art Palace

Course Fees: RMB1,000 for all 4 sessions, ticket not included, or RMB300/session, ticket not included.

Registration: Email Kate at galleryeastlink@gmail.com

I took the course last year (read my posts from 2.14.12 and 2.21.12) and really enjoyed it. Not only did it get me physically to some galleries I'd been wanting to visit but hadn't had time, the course under Greenspan's guidance gave me a framework with which to think about and view contemporary art - something about which, up until I took the course, I had little understanding.
