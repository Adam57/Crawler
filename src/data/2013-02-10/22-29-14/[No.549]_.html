Essential for Airy Dark Moon: Open Spaces

Friday February 8, 2013

Some fresh currents swirl as the Moon goes into Aquarius here shortly (Friday, 2:16 pm EST). Then in the Dark ( balsamic) phase, there are aspects to the Moon that are in a word -- trippy. It's time for moving out of the usual thought loops, and journeying to new places.

A huge accomplishment in our time is to break out of the well-trodden grooves -- like hitting all your bookmarked pages on the net, in the same circuit. It's time for a shake-up, to rearrange or delete files, to eliminate background noise, or unplug altogether.

Being in wide open spaces has that same effect, especially when the air is crisp or forceful. In these hours before the New Moon, take time for activities that let the fresh air in. This is a Twelfth House lunation for me, and I'm feeling the need to retreat a bit, and potter around. Enjoy and I'll catch you on the flip side.

Dark of the Moon - Dream or Nightmare?

Thursday February 7, 2013

Dark Moons pull us into the great mystery, and sometimes that's the hazardous, less well-lit areas of the psyche. This New Moon approaching is in air sign Aquarius, the Waterbearer, for breathing in fresh, freeing currents. But the release and perceptual breakthrough of Aquarius, comes some shadowy debris.

Looking at the New Moon chart, there's a sense of potential catharsis, of crazy genius or just crazy. Be alert to unstable people, and see if you can't carve out time for super stimulating solitude.

I've been having fitful sleep, revisiting the emotional atmosphere of a time in my life that was very dark, and full of secrets. Saturn in Scorpio, the sign of the underworld, is a guide, and is harmonizing with Pisces energy of dissolving (close aspects to Mars-Mercury). I could see the "as above, so below" in my situation, with the two working together to swirl up forsaken parts of who I am, for acceptance and forgiveness. The cool of the air signs makes for clear seeing, into situations that once were murky, stuffed down.

The past can be released in a deeper way, and seen from new angles. There's treasure in these excavations, in the energy released, and in the wisdom gained. I love thinking of dreams as messages from the soul. So a nightmare is a message of a psychic disturbance that's seeking integration somehow.

In the days before a New Moon, I like to look at the chart for close contacts with any planets, and see which house the Sun-Moon are in -- this one is 22 Aquarius. See your forecast for Aquarius New Moon in the Houses. After that kind of brainstorming, I like to just let it go, and see what happens.

See more with updated notes on the Aquarius New Moon. New Moon is late Saturday, February 9th at 11:20 pm (Pacific) and Sunday, February 10th at 2:20 am (Eastern).

"They saw stars as living silver..." Tolkein on the Ancients

Tuesday February 5, 2013

In many ways, we of European descent are somehow cut off from our indigenous roots. And I've long seen Pluto's transit in Capricorn as a path to re-discover the potency of these lineages -- to reanimate the world of matter. It's digging for authentic origins, something ancient and powerful. It's a revival of the real old-time religion.

I came across an inspired interview on Red Ice Radio with author and seeker Robert Tindall, in which he explores the shamanic roots of western culture. It's been awhile since I've heard something that sounded a note of resonance in my own quest for indigenous paths, mainly Celtic. I highly recommend it, and the first hour is free.

Tindall's site is called Roaming the Mind, where he writes about J.R.R. Tolkien work as a sacred text, which can be a literary bridge to the indigenous past.

He quotes Tolkien talking to C.S. Lewis, in an appeal to perceive the animated universe, "You look at trees and call them trees and probably you do not think twice about the word. You call a star a star and think nothing more of it. But you must remember that these words...tree, star...were in their original forms names given to these objects by people with very different views from yours....(paraphrasing)...to them the universe was alive with mythological beings. They saw the stars as living silver bursting into flame in answer to the eternal music." This became the ode Mythopoeia dedicated to Lewis.
