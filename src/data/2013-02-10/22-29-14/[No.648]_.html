When You Start Duct-Taping a Kid's Shoes, the One Whose Behavior Needs Changing Is You

Friday February 8, 2013

What is it about shoes, do you think, that causes educators to become unhinged? Reading about the 8-year-old with Down syndrome in Indianapolis who came home from school with her shoes duct-taped to her socks because staff had apparently had it with her shoe removal or refusal to put shoes back on, I remembered my own family's shoe struggle when my son was in fourth grade. He developed a habit of slipping his sneakers off in the classroom, in response to which a paraprofessional developed a habit of nagging him unceasingly. I've written some thoughts about why these little things get so blown out of proportion (by school personnel, and if we're honest, by parents sometimes too) in a new article, "When Goals Get Out of Control." Has an educator made some little thing about your child into a big deal? Have you? Share in the comments.

No One Really Needs to Use the R-Word

Thursday February 7, 2013

Earlier this week, sitting at a table in the cafeteria at my son's college, I had to give some kid the look -- you know, the one that says, Yes, I heard you just use the R-word, and you and I both know it's not right. It was a quick look; I'm sensitive to being a parent on a college campus and don't want to go around momming everybody. But I heard the kid give an uncomfortable giggle, so I'm pretty sure he knew he was busted, and while the talk from that table continued to be, let's just say, fresh, I didn't hear that particular offensive word again. Seems as though, if you're paying money to go to college, you should make them teach you some more words, don't you think? Maybe some of the ones on my list of alternatives to the R-word. Stretch your vocabulary, fellas.
