A Colfax Valentine

Tuesday February 5, 2013

Send your love a Valentine's Day message on Denver's longest street, Colfax Avenue. The Colfax Business Improvement District (CBID) will string up a red heart with your message on light poles along the busy thoroughfare for $30, and the funds will benefit a Halloween party for children thrown by the Denver Police Department. "Typically messages are simple, such as 'Bill loves Mary.'  There are around 75 hearts available for sale," wrote Stephanie Salazar from the CBID in an email.

Giveaway: Southwest Airlines Tix

Monday February 4, 2013

Southwest Airlines is giving away two roundtrip tickets each day from now until Valentine's Day. Skaters who glide on over to the Southwest Rink at Skyline Park can enter into a daily drawing for the free tickets. The ice skating rink (pictured at right in 2010) is located near the intersection of 16th Street and Arapahoe Street in downtown Denver. The skating rink offers free admission, and skaters can bring their own ice skates or rent a pair for $2. "We're absolutely thrilled that more than 40,000 skaters have come out to the Southwest Rink at Skyline Park this year, and we hope even more Denverites will take to the ice these last two weeks and enter for their chance to win a trip on Southwest Airlines!" said Linda Rutherford, Southwest Airlines vice president of communication and strategic outreach, in a statement.

SIA Snow Show 2013

Monday February 4, 2013

The SnowSports Industries America (SIA) Snow Show attracted thousands of ski and snowboard industry professionals to Denver over the past four days. The Colorado Convention Center transformed into one-stop shopping for all the latest trends for on-mountain gear. While the trade show provided plenty of space for well-known brands such as Elan and Spyder, local Colorado companies such as Rocky Mountain Sunscreen also hawked their wares at the show.

Super Bowl XLVII Preview

Thursday January 31, 2013

Even though the Denver Broncos were eliminated from the playoffs on Jan. 12, football fans in Denver will have a chance to watch a Broncos-less Super Bowl on Sunday. The match-up marks the first time two head coaches that are also brothers have faced off in pursuit of the Vince Lombardi trophy. John Harbaugh coaches the Baltimore Ravens while his brother Jim Harbaugh coaches the San Francisco 49ers. Watch the big game at home, or stop by one of Denver's sports bars that are serving up Super Bowl parties.
