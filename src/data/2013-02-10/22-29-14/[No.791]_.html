Chocolate-Covered February

Sunday February 10, 2013
February is a chocolate-y month in "Chocolatetown USA", and not just for Valentines: "Chocoate-Covered February" has plenty of kid-friendly fun.

Hershey PA is the town named for chocolate magnate Milton Hershey: 3 hours from NYC, 2 from Phillie; big draw is HersheyPark theme park, with coasters and other rides. HersheyPark's not open in winter, but families still enjoy the classic Hotel Hershey (with kids club, and chocolate-themed spa), Hershey Lodge, and Hershey's Chocolate World.

Chocolate-Covered February fun includes a Chocolate Parade, Cody Cocoa Bean Storytelling, Teddy Bear Day, make your one-of-a-kind cupcake at Hershey's Chocolate World, Hershey Mudslide Cookie Demonstration, Afternoon Chocolate Tea at The Hotel Hershey, Chocolate-Covered Children's Tea at Hershey Lodge, and much more. A Family Fun Package on President's Weekend includes a stay at Hershel Lodge plus breakfast, tickets to Hershey attractions, and a complimentary 5 lb. Hershey's Milk Chocolate Bar; price starts at $379 for a family of four.

Chinese New Year All Over the World

Friday February 8, 2013
Would you guess that Chinese New Year is one of the biggest events of the year -- in Paris?

Paris is one of many cities around the globe that's historically had a large Chinese community, and will have a grand parade, as will San Francisco, Queens, and Sydney .... Not to mention the parades and fireworks mega-extravaganzas in China and Hong Kong.

In 2013, we're welcoming in the Year of the Snake. The date itself is Monday February 10 but festivities are spread over several weeks. Many parades take place on the weekends of February 9th and 16th.

If your family's visiting New York, or D.C., or L.A., or another celebrating city on the right date, you can enjoy colorful (free) festivities. See more about Chinese New Year Celebrations in 2012, and see photos from a past Chinese New Year Parade.
