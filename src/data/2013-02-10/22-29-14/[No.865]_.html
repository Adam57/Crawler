Gluten-Free Chocolate Walnut Truffle Cookies

Sunday February 10, 2013

I'm always on the lookout for gluten-free recipes that use less gluten-free flours, starches and sugar than the average recipe - especially cookie recipes. Crowding our diets with refined carbohydrates, gluten-free or not, just isn't very healthy in the long run.

This recipe for gluten-free chocolate walnut truffle cookies was adapted to gluten-free diets from a recipe which I found on the Ghirardelli Chocolate website - "Chocolate Truffle Cookies with Sea Salt." After making a few modifications to the original Ghirardelli recipe, the end result was a delicious brownie-like cookie. I stored my cookies in the refrigerator which I think made them extra chewy delicious.

The recipe makes two dozen gluten-free cookies using only 6 tablespoons of all purpose gluten-free flour, 1/2 cup of cane sugar and 2 tablespoons of butter. Compare that to your average chocolate chip cookie recipe! And they make a batch for your chocolate-loving Valentine!

What is Gluten Reduced Beer?

Saturday February 2, 2013

First there were gluten-free beers like Redbridge from Anheuser-Busch, Bard's Tale and New Grist from Lakefront Brewery - beer-like drinks made with sorghum that contain zero gluten from barley.

Some people like gluten-free beers, some tolerate the unique taste and others think they're horrible.

Today gluten-free beer drinkers have a new option -  "gluten reduced" beer - real beer in the true sense of the definition, this beer is made from malted barley. It's called "gluten reduced" beer due to a specialized enzyme added to the beer which acts to break down the gluten protein in barley into smaller proteins.

Learn more about gluten reduced beers, how they are made, how they are tested for gluten, how current labeling laws affect how they are marketed and ultimately, if they are safe for Celiacs.
