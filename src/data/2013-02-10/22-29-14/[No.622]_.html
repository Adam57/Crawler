How Have You Supported a Loved One During Cancer

Thursday January 31, 2013

When my husband was diagnosed with cancer last year, I wanted to give him the support he needed throughout treatment. I was there for every appointment, surgery, and therapy treatment. I ensured his diet contained foods that would promote healing and were healthy. I changed dressings after surgery and helped him dress when he was too weak. As I write this now, I see I did a lot to support my husband, but at the time, I felt like it wasn't enough. When someone you love has cancer, you do everything it takes to make them a little more comfortable and to increase their quality of life.

Have you supported someone during their cancer diagnosis and/or treatment? What did you do? Share how you supported your loved one. You may inspire others to support their loved ones in different ways! Share you response here.

ESPN's Stuart Scott Suffers Cancer Recurrence

Thursday January 31, 2013

In a devastating post on Twitter, ESPN anchor Stuart Scott announced he was battling cancer once again. Just two weeks ago, Scott posted:

"Blessed by prayers..I'm back in the Fight. C reared its head again. Chemo evry 2 wks but I'll still work, still work out..still #LIVESTRONG"

Scott is no stranger to cancer, having being first diagnosed in 2007 after an emergency appendectomy. After undergoing treatment, the disease remained in remission until it returned in 2010. Now, almost three years later, he is battling cancer once again.

Scott has been extremely candid about his cancer, yet has chosen to keep the type he suffers from private.

10 Cancer Symptoms Women Should Know

Thursday January 31, 2013

Cancer has plagued man for centuries. Even the ancient Egyptians suffered from cancer, though not at the rate we do today. What has changed and continues to change is earlier detection for many types of cancer. We know more about early warning signs and have screening tests that aid in earlier detection. It doesn't appear that cancer as a whole is going to be eradicated anytime soon, so it's important for people to be proactive about their health.

You can start by learning your family's medical history and reviewing it with your doctor. Having regular cancer screening tests like the Pap smear (if you're a woman) and colonoscopy (at the recommended age, of course). You can also start by listening to your body!

Far too many people ignore symptoms they are experiencing or attribute them to something minor. Take the to time educate yourself about disease and conditions you may be at risk for or those that are common. I have created a list of cancer symptoms all women should know that includes information about common symptoms like pelvic pain and when you should worry about them.
