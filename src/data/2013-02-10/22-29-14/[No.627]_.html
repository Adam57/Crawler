Hang Up and Drive

Thursday January 31, 2013

The National Transportation and Safety Board is recommending all 50 states ban all cell phone usage in automobiles. Everything from texting, talking, and even hands free talking is listed in the recommended ban encouraging everyone to hang up the phone and drive. Poll Position has done a survey which estimates half of all American's support the ban proposed by the NTSB.

Distracted driving isn't anything new, however it is definitely growing along with the popularity of smart phones. The poll estimating half of all American's agree with the ban is surprising to me, since when I drive down the road it seems more than half of all drivers are on the phone. It's frustrating a ban is warranted to begin with, but the roadways do seem to be getting scarier. If you spend any length of time driving, usually an erratic driver can be seen talking away on the phone.

The controversy is sure to continue, with the defense of cell phones being all the other distractions a driver deals with on a daily basis. Eating and driving, passengers talking, and kids crying are just a few distractions in a seemingly endless list of distractions. Hands free devices seem very comparable to a passenger in the car, and banning passengers isn't going to happen.

Texting bans are currently in effect in 39 states and the District of Columbia. New York has the stiffest penalties and does not allow hand held device usage by drivers. New York's cell phone traffic law includes 3 points on your license which would in turn affect your car insurance rate. It's clear more and more states are going to be intensifying the penalties making a distracted driver a high risk driver.

Why Insurance with Multiple Carriers is a Bad Idea

Thursday January 24, 2013

From time to time, I get potential new clients in the office with their insurance all over the place. Their autos insured with one company and their home with another. Sometimes family members in the same household have different auto carriers instead of having all vehicles with the same insurance carrier. I always ask them, why do you have your policies separate?

The answers vary. The most common answer is people allow their mortgage broker to obtain home insurance for their new home and they never think to check with their auto insurance carrier. Sometimes family members cannot agree on a single carrier. Regardless what the answer is, it is usually a bad idea to have insurance with multiple carriers.

Top Reasons Multiple Carriers are a Bad Idea
  1. Lose Multi Policy Discount
  2. Lose Multi Car Discount
  3. Hassle with Multiple Bills

Keeping all of your insurance with the same carrier has a lot of benefits. Insurance companies want multiple lines of business and can be more lenient on customers who have all their business with them. For instance, if you have fallen behind on your payments, an insurance company is more likely to work with a client that has their auto, home, and motorcycle policy insured verses a client with just a home policy. The insurance company has more incentive to retain and work with the multi-policy customer.

How to Get Car Insurance Fast

Thursday January 17, 2013

Sometimes it can be a long process shopping for car insurance. If you call an agency, usually you have to give all your information and wait for a call back from the agent with your quote. But, you might need insurance right now and don't have time to wait for a call back from an agent.

2 Guaranteed Ways to Get Car Insurance Fast
  • Grab your current declaration page and go straight to your preferred agent's office. A face to face quote can quickly turn into an insurance policy.
  • The number one perk of online car insurance is speed. Get car insurance on your own time day or night. Print your proof of insurance from home.

Fast car insurance is not necessarily the best car insurance. If you sped through the quote process because of necessity, take the time to review your policy once you have more time. Changes can be made to your policy at anytime and typically you can switch carriers at anytime without penalties.
