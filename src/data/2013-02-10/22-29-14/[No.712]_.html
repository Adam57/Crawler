Grand Circle Russian Waterways M/S Rossia Ship Tour

Sunday February 10, 2013
Cruising the Russian waterways between St. Petersburg and Moscow is a great cruise tour, and I thoroughly enjoyed my sailing on the Grand Circle M/S Rossia river ship.

Grand Circle targets its cruises and tours to the over-50 American traveler, and the company does an excellent job of both educating and entertaining this growing age group. After 16 days in Russia, I came away with some marvelous memories of its cities and countryside. The Grand Circle team greatly facilitated our learning and discovery process, and we all learned much more about this mysterious country than we ever could have done independently.

Personally, I also came away from this cruise with an appreciation of why Grand Circle Travel has so many loyal customers who return time after time to their cruises and tours. This article focuses on the M/S Rossia--its cabins, common areas, outdoor decks, dining, and onboard activities.

Silver Spirit Cruise Travel Journal - Santiago to Acapulco

Friday February 8, 2013
A winter cruise to South and Central America is a great getaway from the cold weather up north.
In addition to the good weather, the Pacific coast of Latin America offers cruise travelers the opportunity to visit fascinating countries filled with natural beauty and historical significance.

Our cruise on the Silversea Cruises' Silver Spirit covered almost 4,000 miles from Santiago (Valparaiso) to Acapulco, stopping in Peru, Ecuador, Costa Rica, and Guatemala. This 540-passenger cruise ship was on one leg of an extended cruise of the Americas, and I loved the size and variety of activities and dining experiences the ship offered. Of course, the exceptional service and accommodations that Silversea is known for were also seen on the Silver Spirit.

This Silver Spirit travel journal covers the experiences we enjoyed on the ship and in the ports of call.

Carnival Liberty Cruise Ship Tour

Thursday February 7, 2013
The Carnival Liberty was the first Carnival Cruise Lines' ship to undergo the FunShip 2.0 enhancement program in late 2011.

I sailed the western Caribbean on the Carnival Liberty, and it was fun to see all the new bars and dining venues on the ship. I loved the two outdoor bars that flank the Lido deck pool and the changes made to the sports bar. Since my husband and I enjoy Mexican food, the new cantina and its adjacent salsa bar were a big hit with us, as was the hamburger stand developed by celebrity chef Guy Fieri.

This comprehensive tour of the Carnival Liberty points out the changes and provides a close-up look at this fun ship.
