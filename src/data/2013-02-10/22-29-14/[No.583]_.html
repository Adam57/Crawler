Monopoly: Cat In, Iron Out

Wednesday February 6, 2013

The people of the world have spoken, and there will be a new Monopoly token: the cat. The feline playing piece will appear in Monopoly games starting later this year.

The cat, which received 31 percent of the vote, beat out four competitors in an online poll: a robot, guitar, helicopter and diamond ring. It will join the battleship, race car, Scottie dog, thimble, top hat, and wheelbarrow.

To make room for the cat, game publisher Hasbro also asked voters to choose which token should be eliminated. In the last days of voting, three were in danger: the shoe, wheelbarrow, and iron. In the end, voters gave the boot to the iron. (The wheelbarrow may have been saved by a campaign organized by Ames True Temper of Camp Hill, Pennsylvania, which manufactures about 80 percent of the wheelbarrows sold in the United States. Meanwhile, the shoe was promoted by online retailer Zappos.)

This is not the first time that Monopoly tokens have been changed. In the early 1950s, the game included a lantern, purse and rocking horse -- until they were replaced by the dog, horse and rider, and wheelbarrow. Other tokens used through the years include an elephant, a cannon (actually, a howitzer), and a sack of money.

According to Hasbro, voters from 120 countries took part in the poll.

Ticket to Ride iPhone App Free

Sunday February 3, 2013

The iPhone version of the brilliant board game Ticket to Ride is free for a limited time. Game publisher Days of Wonder posted on the Ticket to Ride Facebook page that the Ticket to Ride Pocket app will be free until Tuesday, February 5, at midnight Pacific time.

I have the iPad version of the app (which is not free), and it's a terrific rendition of the game. Friends who have the iPhone version say the same thing. The free Ticket to Ride app is available on iTunes.

Forbidden Desert: Sequel to Forbidden Island

Thursday January 31, 2013

Matt Leacock's Forbidden Island was one of my favorite games of 2010, and this year game publisher Gamewright is bringing out a sequel, Forbidden Desert: Thirst for Survival. The new game is the first sequel in Gamewright's catalog, and it will challenge players to "throw caution to the wind and survive both blistering heat and blustering sand in order to recover a legendary flying machine buried under an ancient desert city," according to a post on the company's blog.

Gamewright says it asked Leacock to "create a game that would contain familiar elements while offering up a completely different in-game experience ... while upping the ante for those who felt they had mastered Forbidden Island." I can't wait to give it a shot.

Forbidden Desert, for 2 to 5 players ages 10 and up, is scheduled to be released this spring.

50 Most Significant Games Since 1800

Thursday January 31, 2013

A few years back, I put together a list of what I consider to be the 50 most historically and culturally significant games published since 1800. I've been thinking about updating the list, but while I've been sitting around thinking, Joe Huber has posted his own updated list at The Opinionated Gamers.

We have a lot of games in common, including Kriegspiel (1824) and The Settlers of Catan (1995). But there are also a lot of differences. Joe did a great job with his list, and made some very compelling arguments for the games he included. In a few cases, he might even convince me. (To give just a few examples, he's clearly right about Poker, and I'm inclined to agree on The Lord of the Rings and Dominion.)
