City as a Sanctuary

Sunday February 10, 2013

Many birders enjoy birdscaping to turn their yards into mini-sanctuaries, but the city of Walla Walla, Washington goes even further. According to the Pioneer from Whitman College, the entire city was designated a bird sanctuary a decade ago. Officials recognize the importance of birds to the city's quality of life, and encourage steps to protect birds from cats and non-native eastern fox squirrels. The local National Audubon Society chapter, the Blue Mountain Audubon Society, also works to raise awareness of local birds and encourages residents and visitors alike to enjoy their avian neighbors.

Rehab Facility Rejecting Birds

Saturday February 9, 2013

The Suncoast Seabird Sanctuary has rescued tens of thousands of birds since it opened in 1971, but now it has to say no to new avian guests. According to the Tampa Bay Times, ongoing financial difficulties have forced the facility to stop accepting new birds needing rehabilitation. The sanctuary has tried to overcome its financial issues, but inconsistent leadership, poor fundraising efforts and the ever increasing needs of birds have taken their toll.

Over the years, the sanctuary has worked with birds of more than 155 species, and nine-tenths of the injured birds they receive are hurt from human actions, such as gunshot wounds, tangled in fishing line or inadvertent poisoning. While there are other regional facilities available for injured birds, none is as large, and for now, the help available to injured birds is uncertain.

If you find an injured bird, learn how to contact a bird rescue organization so you can get it the help it needs!

Pennies for Birds

Friday February 8, 2013

Canada is doing away with its penny to save money for the government, and now you can do away with your Canadian pennies and save money for the Orphaned Wildlife Rehabilitation Society in Delta, British Columbia. The facility - which specializes in the rehabilitation of raptors - is holding a penny drive to help raise money for a new facility. What may just be a few pennies to you can really add up to a lot of help for injured birds of prey!

What unique projects have you done to help fulfill a bird rescue wish list, or what birding club fundraisers do you use to help finance projects? Share your ideas in the comments!

Bird of the Week: Gray Catbird

Thursday February 7, 2013

Birders who love cats can't help but love this week's featured bird - the gray catbird has a remarkable "mew" call reminiscent of a plaintive kitten. This bird also mimics other bird calls, nighttime frogs, machinery and more, giving it a wide range of vocalizations that make up its complex songs. That complexity is a sharp contrast to its relatively plain and simple plumage, but put together, this bird is the whole package.

Have you seen - or heard - the gray catbird? Share your experiences in the comments!
