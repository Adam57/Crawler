Be a Good Conversationalist

Sunday February 10, 2013

One key to success in business and in your social life is to be a good conversationalist. Knowing the right thing to say in each situation can do wonders for your professional and social life.

Have you ever gone somewhere and felt very uncomfortable because no one knows what to say? The awkwardness is downright miserable. Be prepared by learning some pointers on starting a conversation so this doesn't happen to you.

I've been around many people who like to talk, but their actual conversation skills leave quite a bit to be desired. Follow these tips and techniques so you don't become that person: Conversation Etiquette.

Don’t Be Late!

Saturday February 9, 2013

Punctuality is one of the most important etiquette concepts you should remember.

Being late for anything is a sign of disrespect. Think about how you feel when you have to wait for someone who isn't at the designated place when he or she is supposed to be.

Whether you are interviewing for a new job or having dinner with friends, being late can put a crimp in the plans. You probably won't get the job, and your friends might hesitate before inviting you to join them in the future if you are a habitual tardy person.

The Fine Art of Regifting Without Embarrassment

Friday February 8, 2013

Over the years, I've received many gifts that I can't use or don't care for. However, I would never want to hurt the giver's feelings. My mother-in-law had a box filled with such items, and she gave me some wonderful tips on how to do it with grace.

She taught me to be very careful not to let anyone know I didn't keep their present. However, she did have one oops moment when she regifted something to me that I'd given her the year before. I'd known her for a long time by then, so I knew she would never want to hurt my feelings. I didn't say anything.

Some people think that regifting isn't polite. My opinion is that it's fine as long as you do it with grace and caution. Follow the guidelines in this article: Regifting Etiquette.
