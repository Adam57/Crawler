A Monumental Manger in Amsterdam

Wednesday January 30, 2013

Horse lovers have their own corner of Amsterdam in the form of the Hollandse Manege (Dutch Manger), the Netherlands' answer to Austria's famed Spanish Riding School in Vienna. Take a ride or just stop in to admire these majestic beasts at their abode on the northeastern corner of the Vondelpark.

Amsterdam's Most Impressive Urban Garden

Monday January 28, 2013

In the dead of winter, experience some tropical heat at the Hortus Botanicus (Botanical Garden) of Amsterdam. Greenhouses that simulate various climates are carefully monitored to sustain the many exotic plant species, some of which were introduced by  the Dutch East India Company as far back as the 17th century, when the Hortus was founded.

Amsterdam for Cat Lovers

Saturday January 26, 2013

Fancy cats?  Het Kattenkabinet, or The Cat Cabinet, is an Amsterdam museum made for enthusiasts of the furry four-footers, filled with feline-themed art in all media. Founded as a memorial to the owner's beloved tabby, the museum now draws cat lovers from all over the world. Find out more about Het Kattenkabinet on Amsterdam Travel.
