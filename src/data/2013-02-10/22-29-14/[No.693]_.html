Burning Bright by Tracy Chevalier

Saturday February 9, 2013

In Burning Bright, historical novelist Tracy Chevalier turns her attention to a tale of eighteenth-century London. The year is 1792 and the Kellaway family has just moved from the wilds of Piddletrenthide, Dorsetshire to this strange urban landscape. Stranger still? The Kellaways' new neighbors are the Blakes - William and Catherine.

Things That Are by Amy Leach

Tuesday February 5, 2013

Amy Leach's Things That Are is a slim treasure trove of poetic naturalist essays with titles that include "Goats, and Bygone Goats" and "Please Do Not Yell at the Sea Cucumber." Leach's lyrical prose it at once funny and insightful as she holds a fantastic lens up to the natural world to reveal insights that just weren't there a minute ago.

The Islands by Carlos Gamerro

Thursday January 31, 2013

The Islands is a plate-spinningly good swirl of Thomas Pynchon, Roberto Bolano and vintage Richard Powers: an absurdist, nationalistic dredge through the Falklands War set in Argentina during the early 90s tech-boom. Felipe Felix is an Argentinian hacker and a veteran of the Falklands War hired by Senor Tamerlan, a wealthy executive, to solve a mysterious murder. Utilizing his technical expertise, Felix is to hack together a list of witnesses and track down any clues relating to the victim and the perpetrator.

The End of Your Life Book Club by Will Schwalbe

Tuesday January 29, 2013

The End of Your Life Book Club is based in a "book club" started and shared by two people - humanitarian Mary Anne Schwalbe and her son Will - after Mary Anne was diagnosed with advanced pancreatic cancer and given just six months to live. This is the true story of how books brought the pair together at the end of Mary Anne's Schwalbe's life.
