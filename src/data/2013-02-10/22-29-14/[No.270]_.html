What's a database?

 

A

  • ABI/INFORM (ProQuest)          
    A business database package that contains over 2,800 full-text journal titles covering business and economic conditions, corporate strategies, management techniques, as well as competitive and product information.
  • AccessEngineering    
    Over 280 books, covering all areas of engineering including: bio, chemical, civil, communications, electrical, environmental, industrial, mechanical, and software engineering. Also includes Schaum's Outlines.
  • AccessScience    
    Fully searchable online edition of McGraw-Hill's Encyclopedia of Science & Technology. Also includes Research Updates from the Yearbooks of Science & Technology, Dictionary of Scientific and Technical Terms, and over 2,000 scientist biographies.
  • American Chemical Society      
    Full text access to 34 research journals in the chemical and related sciences. Includes searchable access to over 130 years of original research in chemistry.
  • American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics (AIAA)    
    Full text access to the current year's AIAA journals featuring original research papers spanning the spectrum of aerospace science and technology.
  • American Institute of Physics (AIP)      
    Full-text access to AIP publications.
  • American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE)  
      
        
    More than 33,000 full-text journal articles dating back to 1993. Bibliographic and abstract records go back to 1970.
  • American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME)  
      
            
    Full text access to ASME's journal archive dating back to 2000. (For full text access, please choose HTML TOC when Browsing.)
  • Annual Reviews      
    Annual Reviews volumes are published each year for 33 focused disciplines within the Biomedical, Physical, and Social Sciences. The full text volumes critically review the most significant primary research literature.
  • ArXiv.org    
    Open access to over 500,000 e-prints in Physics, Mathematics, Computer Science, Quantitative Biology, and other subjects. Provided by Cornell University and the National Science Foundation.
  • Association for Computing Machinery Digital Library (ACM)      
    Citations and full text from ACM journals, some back to 1954.
  • ASTM Standards    
    A vast collection of industry-leading standards and technical engineering information.
 

B

  • Biological Abstracts (ISI Web of Knowledge)      
    An expansive index to the worldâ€™s life sciences journal literature, with topics ranging from biochemistry to microbiology to pharmacology. Includes over 4,200 journal titles, 1969-present.
  • BioMed Central  
    Over 210 peer-reviewed open access journals covering biology and medicine. Browse by subject or conduct an advanced search (free registration required).
  • BioMed Search    
    Biomedical search engine that contains NIH/PubMed documents, plus a large collection of theses, dissertations, and other publications not found anywhere else for free.
  • BIOSIS Previews (ISI Web of Knowledge)      
    Index to life sciences and biomedical research covering pre-clinical and experimental research, methods and instrumentation, biochemistry, biophysics, bioengineering, biotechnology, and more, 1969-present.
  • Books24x7      
    Technology books covering subjects such as C++, Java, object-oriented programming, algorithms, computer networks, cryptography, parallel and distributed computing, and much more.
  • Business Source Premier (EBSCOhost)        
    Provides full text for more than 2,300 journals, including more than 1,100 peer-reviewed titles. Covers all disciplines of business, including marketing, management, MIS, POM, accounting, finance and economics.
 

C

  • Cambridge Books Online      
    Over 3,000 books in science and engineering plus books covering the humanities, social sciences, and medicine.
  • Cambridge Journals Online        
    Full-text access to a wide selection of journals published by the Cambridge University Press in the social sciences, math and sciences.
  • Chicago Manual of Style Online      
    A searchable guide that provides quick answers to your style and editing questions. Covers everything from grammar and usage to manuscript preparation.
  • Chronicle of Higher Education    
    The number one source of news, information, and jobs for college and university faculty members and administrators.
  • Chronicle of Philanthropy    
    The number one news source for fund raisers, grant makers, and other people involved in the philanthropic enterprise.
  • CINAHL Plus (EBSCOHost)      
    Provides indexing for 3,802 journals from the fields of nursing and allied health, with indexing back to 1937. Full text material includes nearly 80 journals plus legal cases, clinical innovations, critical paths, drug records, and research instruments.
  • CiteSeerX  
    A freely available digital library and search engine that focuses primarily on the literature in computer and information science. Includes a citation search feature.
  • Computing Reviews  
      
        
    Provides reviews and overview needed to identify the essential books and articles in computer science.
  • COS Funding Opportunities
    This is a database of more than 30,000 research grants. It allows you to perform highly specialized searches to identify sources of funding with application dates that are still open.
 

D

  • Directory of Open Access Journals  
    Includes free, full text, quality controlled scientific and scholarly journals covering all subjects and languages.
 

E

  • EBSCOhost        
    A variety of full text databases ranging from general reference collections to subject-specific databases. Select EBSCOhost Web to search multiple databases at once.
  • Economist Intelligence Unit  
      
          
    Covers over 200 markets, including country analysis and forecasts, risk assessments, economic and market data, and industry trends. Frequency of updates vary, with most occurring monthly or annually.
  • Encyclopedia Britannica Online      
    Full-text access to the encyclopedia, with multimedia available for some articles, plus online access to the 10th edition of Merriam-Webster's Collegiate Dictionary.
  • Engineering Village  
      
            
    Includes Compendex- a citation and abstract database of scientific and technical engineering research and Referex - full text engineering reference e-books
  • Environment Complete (EBSCOhost)      
    An interdisciplinary database that contains more than 1,957,000 records from more than 1,700 domestic and international titles going back to the 1940s. The database also contains full text for more than 680 journals and 120 monographs.
 

G

  • Gale Group        
    Collection of general reference databases with specialized collections on a wide variety of topics including literary research, company information, Spanish language periodicals and newspaper full text articles and indexing.
  • GoogleScholar  
      
    Peer-reviewed papers, theses, books, preprints, abstracts, patents, and technical reports from broad areas of research.
  • GreenFILE (EBSCOhost)        
    This bibliographic database draws on the connection between the environment and disciplines such as technology, health, education, and law. Full text is available for some titles.
 

H

  • Hindawi Publishing  
    Open access journals in the fields of engineering, life sciences, mathematics, and physical sciences.
  • Hoover's Company Profiles (ProQuest)      
    Contains proprietary information about more than 40,000 public and non-public companies and 225,000 key executives. Hoover's delivers in depth industry analyses, information on summary financials, top competitors, top officers and more.
 

I

  • IEEE Xplore      
    Full text access to the world's highest quality technical literature in electrical engineering, computer science, and electronics. Includes access to IEEE journals, proceedings, and standards, with select content back to 1952.
  • Ingenta Connect Complete    
    A citation database that covers research articles in a wide range of fields.
  • Inspec (ISI web of Knowledge)      
    A comprehensive index to the global journal and conference proceedings in physics, electrical/electronic engineering, computing, control engineering, and information technology, 1898-present.
  • Institute of Physics (IOP)      
    Full text journals in Physics, Materials Science, and Math.
  • IRIS
    The Illinois Researcher Information Service is a free database of funding opportunities in the sciences and the humanities provided by the University of Illinois.
  • ISI Web of Knowledge      
    A collection of bibliographic databases in the sciences, social sciences, and humanities. Includes: Web of Science, Biological Abstracts, BIOSIS Previews, Inspec, MEDLINE, and Journal Citation Reports.

J

  • JSTOR      
    Interdisciplinary full text archives of over one thousand leading academic journals across the humanities, social sciences, and sciences, as well as select monographs and other materials. The most recently published issues (past 3-5 years) are not available
  • Journal Citation Reports (ISI Web of Knowledge)    
    Delivers quantifiable statistical information on a journal's impact based on citation data. Provides a variety of impact and influence metrics, including the Journal Impact Factor and Eigenfactor.
 

K

  • Knovel  
      
          
    Online science and engineering reference collection that includes material properties, process and design information, best practices, equations, and interactive graphs and tables.
 

L

  • Lexis Nexis Academic      
    Provides full-text access to over 6,000 news, business, and legal sources. Includes deep backfiles and up-to-the-minute stories in national and regional newspapers, and non-English language sources.
  • Lexis Nexis Congressional      
    Ideal for in-depth research in public policy, historical, and legal areas of interest. Find congressional materials, voting records and legislative histories.
 

M

  • MathSciNet      
    Reviews, abstracts and bibliographic information for much of the mathematical sciences literature.
  • MEDLINE (ISI Web of Knowledge)      
    The premier index of the U.S. National Library of Medicine covers biomedicine and life sciences, bioengineering, clinical care, and plant and animal science from 1950-present.
 

N

  • New Scientist Archive (EBSCOHost)      
    Features full text as well as indexing and abstracting for all weekly issues of the magazine from 1997 to present.
 

O

  • OAIster  
      
      
    Provides free access to millions of digital resources from open archive collections worldwide. Types of resources include research papers, audio files, images, theses, and technical reports in a wide variety of subjects.
  • Organic Syntheses    
    Detailed and reliable procedures for the synthesis of organic compounds. Some procedures describe practical methods for the preparation of specific compounds of interest, while other procedures illustrate important synthetic methods with general utility.
  • Oxford English Dictionary      
    More than 2.5 million entries illustrating how words are used.
  • Oxford University Press Journals      
    Full-text access to over 200 academic and research journals in Life Sciences, Mathematics, Physical Sciences, Medicine, Social Sciences, Humanities, and Law.
 

P

  • Physical Review Online Archive      
    The American Physical Society's online archive for Physical Review Letters, Reviews of Modern Physics, and Physical Review Aâ€“E. It contains all APS journal articles published from 1893â€“2004. Each January, another year is added to the archive.
  • PLOS  
    Public Library of Science (PLOS) publishes open access journals across the range of science and medicine.
  • ProQuest        
    A large multidisciplinary database with over 11,000 titles (over 8,000 in full-text). Over 160 subjects areas are covered extensively including business and economics, health and medical, news and world affairs, technology, social sciences and more.
  • PsycNET (APA)      
    Search multiple databases at once that include full text books, articles, and gray literature published by the American Psychological Association and other publishers in subject areas such as applied psychology, health, theory, research, social/personality, and more.
  • PubGet    
    A search engine that links you directly to the PDF of an article. The scope focuses primarily on the life sciences.
  • PubMed      
    More than 19 million citations for biomedical articles from MEDLINE and life science journals. Citations may include links to full-text articles from PubMed Central or publisher web sites.
 

R

  • Regional Business News (EBSCOHost)      
    Provides comprehensive full-text coverage for regional business publications, including 75 business journals, newspapers and newswires from all metropolitan and rural areas within the United States.
  • Royal Society of Chemistry    
    Access to over 20 full text journals covering all branches of chemistry, including inorganic, organic, analytical, physical and chemical physics. In addition, the archive includes all articles published by the Royal Society of Chemistry from 1841 to 2004
 

S

  • Safari Books Online      
    Over 280 technical e-books in computer science and information technology, including programming languages, operating systems, software applications, and much more.
  • SAGE      
    SAGE publishes more than 485 journals in the Life, Social & Physical Sciences,Technology, Medicine, Business, and Humanities. To only retrieve results that are available in full text, go to advance search and select "SAGE Full-Text Collections."
  • Science Citation Index  
    See "Web of Science"
  • Science Direct (Elsevier)        
    Full text access to thousands of peer-reviewed journals, books, and reference works in a wide range of disciplines including Life Sciences, Physical Sciences, Engineering, Math, Health, Management, Social Sciences and the Humanities.
  • Scitation    
    More than one million links to documents from scholarly journals, magazines, conference proceedings, and other special publications from scientific societies. Some full text available.
  • Scopus      
    "World's largest" abstract and citation database covering many disciplines. Can use citation tracker to see how many citations an article or an author has received.
  • Smithsonian Physical Tables    
    Classic reference source comprises 901 tables of general interest to scientists and engineers, and of particular interest to those involved with physics. Full-text searchable electronic format makes data easily accessible.
  • Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics (SIAM) Journals  
      
        
    Full-text access to select peer-reviewed research journals published by SIAM (click "i" for titles).
  • Springer Materials: Landolt-Bornstein Database      
    Consists of 91,000 online documents and 165,000 substances and material systems. It is based on Landolt-Bornstein, a compilation of all verified physical data resulting from research in the fields of physics, chemistry, astronomy, geophysics, and technology.
  • SpringerLink      
    A full text online collection of scientific, technological and medical journals, books and reference works. Most journal titles include complete back issues from 1997.
  • Synthesis Digital Library of Computer Science and Engineering      
    A full-text collection covering 30 engineering areas, primarily related to electrical engineering, computer science, and bioengineering. It contains relatively brief introductions (50-250 pages) to complex topics.
 

T

  • Theses      
    Download full-text theses and dissertations from NYU-Poly and other schools nationwide. Scroll down and select "Dissertations & Theses" (part of ProQuest databases)
 

W

  • Web of Science (ISI Web of Knowledge)  
      
          
    Access leading scholarly literature in the sciences, social sciences, arts, and humanities and examine proceedings of international conferences, symposia, seminars, colloquia, workshops, and conventions.
  • Wiley Online Library      
    An extensive multidisciplinary collection of online resources, covering life, health and physical sciences, social science, and the humanities.
  • WorldCat Advanced  
    Search millions of records in libraries worldwide. Click "Request this Item Now" to get items not available in Dibner Library through interlibrary loan.
  • WorldCat.org
    The publicly accessible version of World Cat which offers an easy to use interface and allows you to create a personal profile, share lists, and manage references with ease.
