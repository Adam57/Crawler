Thrifty Thursday: Make Your Own Breakfast

Thursday February 7, 2013

You know, your mama was right: It really is important to eat breakfast. You end up having more energy and eating better (and less) throughout the day, no matter how crazy your college schedule is. But with everything else going on in school, who has the time to deal with finding something to eat as you're rushing out the door?!

If you're watching your nickels, eating a smart, healthy breakfast that you make yourself can end up saving you some serious cash. Sure, those muffins at the coffee shop are easy and tasty -- but they're also expensive and not super healthy.

If you need some fresh ideas on morning munchies, check out my list of 15 Breakfast Ideas for College Students. Because, after all, if you really want that morning muffin, it's way cheaper to make (or even buy) a bunch on your own than it is to fork over your hard-earned cash to the campus coffee shop.

(Here on the About.com College Life blog, every Thursday is "Thrifty Thursday." I'll post a tip each week about how to save money while you're in school. It might be something big; it might be something small. But given how important each nickel is, each little tip adds up, right?)

Campus Highlight: Saint Anselm College's Koinonia Society

Wednesday February 6, 2013

You may have noticed that this week's Campus Highlight is late. I'll spare all you kind readers the details, but let's just say that the flu swept through my house. And it was ugly.

Fortunately, I have the resources available to access medical care ... but I know that improving the health of our communities is an important issue. For this week's Campus Highlight, then, I'm choosing to focus on a student organization that works to provide resources for those in need.

At Saint Anselm College (Manchester, NH), the Koinonia Society's "main function is to work within the local community as an independent service organization. Annually, the women assist the Halloween Party at 'Our Place' (a center for battered women), the Children's' Fairs, Special Olympics Swim Meet, and provide care packages during finals." They are also sponsoring a "Run with Your Heart" 5K on February 10, where "100% of the proceeds benefit cancer fundraising through Relay for Life!"

Keep up the good work, Hawks! And good luck with your race!
