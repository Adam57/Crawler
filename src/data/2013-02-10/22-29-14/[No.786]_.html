Spotlight Crafts - Fortune Cookie Craft, Finger Cymbals, More

Saturday February 9, 2013
This week's craft include ideas for Chinese New Year and Valentine's Day along with some other fun projects.
  • Easy Paper Lantern - Learn how to make simple paper lanterns you can use to decorate for Chinese New Year, which starts on February 10th, or Valentine's Day.

  • Make Your Own Paper Beads - Discover how easy it is to craft colorful beads out of strips cut from old magazines and catalogs.

  • Paper Plate Heart Pocket - This is a fun Valentine's Day craft for the entire family to make. Follow these directions and make a heart-shaped pocket out of paper plates.

  • Fortune Cookie Craft - Not only can you make these cute fortune cookie to help celebrate Chinese New Year, you can also make them to give out on Valentine's Day.

  • Ribbon Flower Craft - Discover how easy it is to craft colorful flowers out of ribbon. You can make an entire bouquet of flowers to give to your sweetheart or use them as decorations.

  • Finger Cymbals - This is a fun musical instrument you can help your kids make using 2 baby food jar lids and some elastic.

  • To Do List Magnet - If you have some scrabble tiles that no longer belong to a game, consider making these magnets out of them. You can also buy scrabble tiles from a craft store.

Enjoy, and remember, if you complete any of these crafts, take a few moments to send me a picture and it can be featured here too.

Book Review: My Felted Friends

Thursday February 7, 2013
I have always been fascinated by needle felting, but I have never had the urge to try it. Maybe it is because I sew, but I just couldn't wrap my head around how you could make a three-dimensional object that looked stuffed without using a sewing machine.

I changed my mind once I read this book. Not only were the directions easy to follow, the projects were too adorable to resist. Read through my review of this book and see if you feel the same way.

Chinese New Year for Kids

Wednesday February 6, 2013
The Chinese New Year will start in less than a week! In 2013, the Chinese New Year starts on February 10th, and it is the Year of the Snake.

In Chinese culture, people born in the Year of the Snake are thought to have a way with money and are supposed to be the most mysterious of all the signs. If you want to celebrate the Chinese New Year, use these craft and activity ideas to help you. There are many fun and creative ways you can celebrate this day.
