Oven Fried Zucchini

Sunday February 10, 2013
This oven fried zucchini recipe is a fantastic alternative to fried zucchini (much less messy and easier to clean up), and the kids will absolutely love it.

This recipe is also a great way to introduce picky eaters to vegetables. Try these vegetable recipes for picky eaters, too:

  • Broccoli Cheese Rice Casserole - Jarred salsa con queso makes this dish easy to prepare, and kids love it!

  • Crispy Kale - Kale is so healthy, and this is a great way for kids to enjoy it.

  • Roasted Green Beans - My kids think these green beans taste like French fries!

Valentine's Day Drinks

Sunday February 10, 2013
From frozen hot chocolate ( just like the kind you get at Serendipity 3 in New York) to chocolate strawberry smoothies (like a chocolate covered strawberry in a glass), these Valentine's Day drinks make festive, non-alcoholic treats for the kids.

Kiss Cookies

Saturday February 9, 2013
What better way to show your love on Valentine's Day than with these adorable kiss cookies?

After all, kids love Hershey kisses. And the cookies in this collection are fun to make, too. Even the littlest kids can press a Hershey kiss into a cookie fresh out of the oven, right?

Try changing things up by using dark chocolate kisses or caramel-filled kisses for some of these recipes. Around Valentine's Day, you can also sometimes find raspberry kisses, which are da bombe!

Chinese Food for Kids

Saturday February 9, 2013
In honor of Chinese New Year, which is tomorrow, I thought I'd share with you some Chinese food recipes for kids. Make these recipes to celebrate the New Year or just make them for fun. They include all sorts of flavors and bright colors that appeal to kids.

Take my beef and broccoli stir fry. This recipe is a symphony of colors that will brighten your plate and your palate. Be sure to cut the beef against the grain for the most tender results.

Fried rice is always popular with kids, and it's a great way to use leftover rice, meats and vegetables. My beef fried rice is always a hit with the little ones.

Want a fun Chinese Crockpot recipe? Try my Asian beef short ribs. These ribs are melt-in-your-mouth tender, and the recipe takes less than 10 minutes of active time to prepare.
