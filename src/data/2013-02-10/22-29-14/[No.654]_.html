Sunday Scripture: Luke 18:1

Sunday January 20, 2013

Luke 18:1 - "One day Jesus told his disciples a story to show that they should always pray and never give up." (NLT)

Never give up. We often hear about failure like it's the worst thing that could ever happen to us, but truthfully, failure can do a lot to build us up. We can use it to motivate ourselves. While failure can sometimes be discouraging, God tells us that there are times to keep trying. We need to keep coming back to Him in prayer when we feel ourselves backsliding. We need to come to Him when we think things are dark and that we'll never achieve our goals.

Just because you try something and it doesn't work out, it doesn't mean the end of the world. It could mean it wasn't the right path for you. It could mean it's just not the right time. It could be that some part of what you tried gave you some skill, knowledge, or idea you needed. God has ways of using our successes and our failures to build us up. We just need to turn to Him and rely on Him, and success will find us, one way or another.

Sunday Scripture: Revelations 3:20

Sunday December 9, 2012

Revelations 3:20 - "Here I am! I stand at the door and knock. If anyone hears my voice and opens the door, I will come in and eat with that person, and they with me." (NIV)

We so often ask God to work in our lives and then slam the door in His face, because we tend to get so wrapped up in ourselves we don't recognize His work. One of the best ways we can see God working is by spending time with Him so we know what to expect. That's not always easy in our busy lives. Everybody seems to have something else to do before they spend time with God.

However, just like we make time for homework and chores, just like we make time for family and friends, we have to make time for God. There are so many moments in the day that we can take for God. From when you first wake up and just wait for the snooze alarm to come on. When you're in the shower lathering your hair. When you're waiting in traffic. All of these times can be given to God. When you make time for God in your life, you offer Him a way into your life.
