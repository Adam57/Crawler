Missouri Latest State to Attack Evolution Education

Wednesday January 16, 2013

Teaching evolution in the public classroom is being challenged by a newly introduced bill in the Missouri House of Representatives. House Bill 179 discusses teaching "controversial subjects" in science classrooms including "biological and chemical evolution". It claims to "endeavor to assist teachers to find more effective ways to present the science curriculum where it addresses scientific controversies" and help students find "scientific weaknesses of the theory of biological and hypotheses of chemical evolution."

While this bill is designed to make it seem as if it is giving children the chance to develop critical thinking skills, it's really just hiding the fact it is actually there to try to plant seeds of doubt about the factual evidence surrounding evolution. This sort of legislation is forcing teachers to teach evolution as if it is controversial when, in the scientific community, it is not controversial at all. When will politicians allow science teachers do their jobs and teach science and then let families decide to teach religion or other "conflicting" viewpoints? I sincerely hope this bill is either completely retooled or entirely discarded. Science education in the United States does not need to take a step backward, as this bill will do to students in the state of Missouri.

New Orleans Bans Teaching Creationism in Schools

Friday December 21, 2012

In a surprising turn of events, a school district in Louisiana has outright banned the teaching of Creationism in the science classroom. The New Orleans School District voted to prohibit the teaching of Creationism in public schools. This is a big win for science in a state where the legislature has pushed to inclusion of Creationism and Intelligent Design in the public science classes.

I should definitely point out that none of the public schools in the New Orleans school district are currently teaching the non-science curriculum, but the vote by the school board to ban it makes sure that it will not happen. Now the debate will continue at the state level and whether or not a statewide mandate will overrun the local school district's wishes. I expect there will be some upcoming court cases and political struggles over this very topic.

To read more about this decision, check out the school board meeting minutes.
