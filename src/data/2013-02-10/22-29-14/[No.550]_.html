Atlanta Gets a New Brewery

Wednesday February 6, 2013

Have you heard about the new brewery in town? Monday Night Brewing, a locally made beer company that's been featured on tap at bars around town for some time now, has opened up their very own brewery.

The quirky space includes a wall of neckties ($1 off your tasting is you bring one to donate!), shuffleboard, festive decor, a firepit, and a whole lotta delicious beer! How's that for some locally crafted goodness?

Tastings are just $10 and include a pint glass. Check them out Thursdays and Fridays from 5:30 - 7:30 p.m. and Saturdays from 2:00 - 4:00 p.m. each week.

Monday Night is just one of the local breweries worth visiting around town, and we expect to see several more popping up before the end of 2013.

Food & Wine Festival Tickets On Sale Soon

Tuesday February 5, 2013

The Atlanta Food & Wine Festival is one of the can't miss food events in this town.  You'll find loads of tasting opportunities, a plethora of booze, and the best learning sessions around from the South's biggest chefs. This is one of the most education-focused food events I've ever attended, with approachable seminars for amateurs and enthusiasts in everything from wine pairing to canning.

Save the date for early bird pricing to the 2013 festival - happening February 14 (Valentine's Day gift, anyone?) through March 18. You can save at every ticket level, and you get especially hefty savings on the pricier passes!

The 2013 Atlanta Food & Wine Festival is taking place from May 30 - June 2 in Midtown.

A Pork Lover's Dream

Thursday January 31, 2013

This goes way beyond bacon, folks. Cochon 555, a traveling culinary competition and tasting event created to promote sustainable farming of heritage breed pigs, lands in Atlanta on February 17. The event features five chefs, five pigs, and five wineries engaging in some delicious head-to-tail pork preparations.

The Atlanta lineup includes Ryan Smith (Empire State South), Joseph Schafer (King and Duke), Eric Ottensmeyer (Leon's and Brickstore), Whitney  Otawka (Farm 255) and Guy Wong (Miso Izakaya). Guests will indulge in an epic pork feast alongside wines from five small family-owned wineries.  Judges and the guests help decide the winning chef, who will go on to compete against other regional winners at the finale Grand Cochon event at the FOOD & WINE Classic in Aspen (you know, that one you hear about on Top Chef).

Tickets are $125 for general admission or $200 for VIP. VIP admission includes a cocktail competition, Tartare Bar, sustainable oysters and reserve wines and spirits.

Mardi Gras in Atlanta

Wednesday January 30, 2013

Mardi Gras comes early this year! Fat Tuesday is just a few weeks away, on February 12. Grab some beads, hit the town, and party the (Tuesday) night away at these Atlanta Mardi Gras events.

From live jazz music to crawfish and jambalaya, Atlanta restaurants and bars will throw down for Fat Tuesday and the surrounding days with big bashes celebrating the Big Easy.
