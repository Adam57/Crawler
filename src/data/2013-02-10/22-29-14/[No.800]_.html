Happy Chinese New Year! Focus Only on Happy, Fresh and Joyful Energy for the Next Two Weeks

Sunday February 10, 2013

Today is the beginning of a 15 day celebration of Chinese New Year. Also called Lunar New Year, Chinese New Year comes on a different day each year because it happens on the first New Moon of the year. This celebration is considered the beginning of Spring.

Even if you do not plan to celebrate Chinese New Year, know that the energies are quite powerful right now - honestly more powerful than during the time we usually think of as the beginning of the year. So, here is another change to thoughtfully and mindfully clarify your goals, then tune into the best energy to help them manifest.

As with any New Moon, it is good to start with a clean slate/clear mind and an open heart. Allow yourself to receive all the beauty that life has in store for you and do your best to stay present and happy.

Having your house clean, happy and ready to welcome fresh energy full of new possibilities is always helpful, too. Make your space beautiful and joyful, bring fresh flowers, play healing music, let go of whatever troubled you in the past and open up to the possibility of a completely new and beautiful year ahead of you!

I like the fact that Chinese New Year celebration is extended over a two week time period. It is not only about sowing good seeds, but also caring for them in the best manner. During these two weeks of Chinese New Year celebration the focus is only on auspicious, joyful and abundant energy.

We've all heard it many times - "What you focus on expands" - so why not spend the next two weeks emphasizing only the positive, the beautiful and the sacred?

Happy Chinese New Year and A Beautiful 2013 to you!
