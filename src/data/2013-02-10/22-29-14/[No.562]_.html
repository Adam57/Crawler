What's the longest baby name?

Thursday January 31, 2013

So, I've been combing through the current most popular baby name charts and grouping names of similar syllabic lengths together. How many syllables do you think the longest name has? And would you guess it is a girl's name or a boy's name?

Well, if you said six syllables and a boy's name, you'd be right. In the top 1000 baby names, there is but one six syllable name, and that name would be....

(drum roll, please)

Maximiliano

Two brownie points to whomever guessed it!

Now I grouped names of similar pronunciation together, but here is the skinny on the number of names based on syllable length.

  • 142 One Syllable Girl Names
  • 112 One Syllable Boy Names
  • 339 Two Syllable Girl Names
  • 458 Two Syllable Boy Names
  • 256 Three Syllable Girl Names
  • 161 Three Syllable Boy Names
  • 52 Four Syllable Girl Names
  • 28 Four Syllable Boy Names
  • 1 Five Syllable Girl Name
  • 1 Five Syllable Boy Name
  • 0 Six Syllable Girl Name
  • 1 Six Syllable Boy Name

Any surprises?

How Many Ellas Do You Know?

Wednesday January 30, 2013

I swear that about 5 to 6 years ago, I didn't know a single woman (or child) named Ella. I mean, sure there was Ella Fitzgerald, but I can't hardly say I knew her. Times have changed though, and I can count more than a dozen little girls named Ella.

I think part of the rise in popularity of Ella is that it was a name riding on the coattails of the immensely popular names Emma and Emily. Ella became a less common an alternative to those names, and so happened to find her own increased popularity because of it.

World-wide, the name Ella is popular in several other countries, and not just English speaking one. England/Wales, Canada, Australia, Belgium, Denmark, Sweden, Ireland, and Scotland currently feature the name Ella on their top 100 name lists.

Why is the Name Elijah Popular?

Wednesday January 30, 2013

You ever notice how certain names seem to come out of no where? A name that you rarely heard before suddenly seems to be named by every other baby you meet? Such seems to be the case with the name Elijah, and I have a theory as to why (see link for details).

Right around 1995 the name started to gain speed and by 2001 it was definitely gaining steam. Last year, it reached its highest point yet on the charts at rank #13. My guess is that the name is going to level off in popularity over the next few years, but time will only tell!
