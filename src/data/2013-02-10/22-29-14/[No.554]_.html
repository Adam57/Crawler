Swimming and Cycling Nude

Thursday February 7, 2013

On February 17, some 200 naked men and women swimmers take part in the Sydney Skinny, the first nude ocean swim which starts at Sydney's Middle Head and covers a distance of 900 metres.

On March 3, Melbourne's Naked Bike Race is scheduled to take place.

These are two Australian events in which men and women are able to participate sans clothing.

Otherwise, don't go nude except at designated nude beaches.

Australia's Smallest Mainland State but There's Much to See and Do in Victoria

Wednesday February 6, 2013

It may be Australia's smallest mainland state but Victoria has much to brag about — just don't tell New South Wales, or any other Australian state or territory, that.

Actually, the smallness of Victoria's land area works to its advantage in that visitor destinations are close to one another, and to Melbourne, so it's more than just feasible to drive from one place to another.

Vineyards are close to Melbourne and two of the state's most popular attractions — Phillip Island and the penguin parade and the Great Ocean Road to the Twelve Apostles — are just a couple of hours' drive over scenic roads.

As for major events, well, there are the world-class Australian Open, the Melbourne Cup and surfing competitions at Bells Beach.

And yes, there's more to see and do...

A is for Australia, B is for...

Saturday February 2, 2013

Brisbane. The capital of Queensland, the city of Brisbane is quite often overlooked as a visitor destination as the more popular Sunshine Coast to its north and Gold Coast to its south tend to be the preferred places to visit. And there's the Great Barrier Reef as well all along the north Queensland coast.

Interestingly, both popular and overlooked places are able to provide more satisfying visitor experiences. It all depends on what people look for.

Check out the ABC of Australian destinations as well as interesting facets of geography, life and culture.

The Sapphires Named Best Film at AACTA Awards

Wednesday January 30, 2013

The 2013 AACTA (Australian Academy of Cinema and Television Arts) Awards, held in Sydney on January 30, recognised outstanding achievements in 2012 in film and television.

Winning the Best Film award, and 10 others besides, was The Sapphires, a critical and popular success and the highest grossing Australian film in 2012.

Acting awards went to The Sapphires' lead actress (Deborah Mailman), lead actor (Chris O'Dowd) and supporting actress (Jessica Mauboy).

Director Wayne Blair received the award for film direction.

The Sapphires tells the story of an indigenous girl band in the 1960s who gained fame with their music and went to Vietnam to entertain the troops.
