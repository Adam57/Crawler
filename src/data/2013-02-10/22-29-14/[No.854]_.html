Obama to Propose Small COLA for Feds

Sunday February 10, 2013

President Obama will propose a 1.0% cost-of-living adjustment for federal employees when he releases his annual budget proposal. While his budget has no actual power, it is a signal to Congress and the general public what his priorities are. Congress must create its own budget that will go to the President to sign -- if they actually pass a budget. Congress does not have a good track record of actually passing a budget over the last few years.

While Congressional Democrats as a whole will take their cues from Obama's budget, Congressional Republicans will take note of it and do their own thing. This is a common practice for the two parties. The roles switch depending on the President's party.

Last time Obama made a budget proposal, it included a 0.5% raise for federal employees. Ultimately, a budget was not passed, and the raise proposal died. The doubling of the raise this time is a great sign for federal employees.

HR Strategist Positions Coming to OPM

Thursday February 7, 2013

The US Office of Personnel Management announced it will repurpose some of its human capital officers. In their new roles, these individuals will focus on broader issues than they had been in their HCO roles. Federal agencies were notified of the change in late January.

"The HCOs will shift their focus from servicing a specific list of agencies, to driving innovative new practices across government through pilot projects that address the Administration's most critical priorities. Changing their name to 'HR Strategist,' OPM is asking these HR professionals to partner with you to design, implement and assess pilot projects in areas such as skills gap closure, workforce restructuring, and other priority areas of HR," the announcement memo says.

TSA Under EEOC Investigation

Wednesday February 6, 2013

On the heels of a report issued by the US Department of Homeland Security's Inspector General, the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission will investigate complaints of favoritism and hostile work environment at the Transportation Security Administration.

As any organization would say, TSA has said it does not tolerate these activities. However, the DHS IG's report called the practicies "rampant," so we will all have to wait and see what the EEOC has to say. The EEOC investigation will not start for several months because of competing priorities and the small size of the staff dedicated to this type of investigation.

You can read more in a Government Executive story.

Agencies Diverting Attention to Sequestration Planning

Tuesday February 5, 2013

Congress is not giving any indication that it will reach a deal to avoid sequestration set to go into effect March 1, 2013. If the fiscal cliff deal is any indication, we will be waiting for quite some time. Federal government agencies cannot wait for Congress to act; they must begin planning for the worst.

Planning for sequestration means that agency leaders and human resources departments cannot do the work that they would normally be doing. This severely cuts into the overall productivity of the organization. If a March 1 sequestration can be avoided, agencies will still probably experience budget cuts, so this planning won't be a total waste.

While agency leaders are frustrated with impending budget cuts of unknown amounts, employees and their vocal unions are unhappy about the threat of furloughs. Sending employees home on unpaid leave is a morale killer, so leaders do not want to do it and will consider all other options before doing so.
