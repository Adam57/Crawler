Children's Books News - 2/9/13

Saturday February 9, 2013
2013 Award Winners - Bank Street College of Education has announced its 2013 award winners: Wonder by R.J. Palacio, Beyond Courage: The Untold Story of Jewish Resistance During the Holocaust and National Geographic Book of Animal Poetry: 200 Poems with Photographs that Squeak, Soar, and Roar!, edited by J. Patrick Lewis.

Hooray for Libraries! - It's National Libraries Day in the UK. In the US, February is Library Lovers' Month. In addition to all the reasons to support and enjoy your public library that I included in my article Save Money at Your Public Library, Esther Lombardi, the About.com Classic Literature Guide, provides some others in her article Reasons to Visit the Library.

Books for Black History Month - School Library's Journal's Doers and Dreamers | Celebrating Black History provides information and cover art for 9 recommended books, including Spirit Seeker, the story of jazz musician John Coltrane, which is on my personal list of the 12 Best Illustrated Children's Books of 2012.

Recommended Books About African American Freedom Fighters

Friday February 8, 2013
Celebrate Black History Month with children's books. Ten women who made a difference in the fight for civil rights are featured in Let It Shine: Stories of Black Women Freedom Fighters, which was written by Andrea Davis Pinkney and illustrated by Stephen Alcorn. It's an excellent book for 9-12 year olds, as well as young teens. Each of the biographies features a dramatic oil on canvas portrait that contains a number of allegorical images. See my African American Freedom Fighters reading list for information about others who made a difference. For additional recommendations, see my February Calendar of Children's Books. Are there books about African American history that your kids particularly like? Click on "Comments" below and share their recommendations.

Rapunzel's Revenge - A Graphic Novel

Thursday February 7, 2013
Non-traditional versions of familiar fairy tales tend to be popular with kids. A version of a fairy tale with elements of a western done in graphic novel format is apt to be even more popular. Make it based on the story of Rapunzel and you have Rapunzel's Revenge, by Shannon and Dean Hale, with illustrations by Nathan Hale, recommended for kids in middle school and younger, ages 10-14. Another good graphic novel for this age group is A Wrinkle in Time: The Graphic Novel, an adaptation of the classic novel by Madeleine L'Engle. For more about graphic novels, see What do Comic Books, Comic-style Books, and Graphic Novels Have in Common? and What Exactly is a Graphic Novel?

A Trio of Board Books for Valentine's Day

Wednesday February 6, 2013
Share Valentine's Day board books with your little ones. Three of my favorites are Max's Valentine by Rosemary Wells, My Fuzzy Valentine, which features Elmo and his Sesame Street friends, and Lilly's Chocolate Heart by Kevin Henkes. For recommended Valentine's Day picture books and beginning readers, see my Top 10 Children's Books for Valentine's Day.
