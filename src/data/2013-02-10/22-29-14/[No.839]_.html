Geography of Marriage

Sunday February 10, 2013

One husband and one wife, multiple wives and one husband, or multiple husbands and one wife - Claire Weber writes in her fascinating article geography of marriage about the variation of partnerships across human cultures and how geography impacts monogamy or polygamy.

Measuring Gross National Happiness

Saturday February 9, 2013

National Public Radio's Planet Money team reported yesterday that the United States is considering measuring its Gross National Happiness. Gross National Happiness is a holistic measure of the well-being of a nation. It has been used by Bhutan since 2004 and has spread to other countries since its inception. Contributing writer Amanda Briney has written an overview of Gross National Happiness that should provide an enlightening and spiritually uplifting experience as you learn about GNH. :)

The World Bank

Saturday February 9, 2013

The World Bank is an international organization with a goal of reducing poverty in middle income nations and supporting the growth of the world's developing nations. In order to meet these goals the World Bank's members have formed an international financial institution that works to provide loans and support to these nations.

Geography at Harvard

Tuesday February 5, 2013

In the latter half of the 20th century, geography as an academic discipline suffered greatly, especially in American higher education. The reasons for this are undoubtedly many, but the biggest contributor was arguably a decision made at Harvard University in 1948 in which university President James Conant declared geography to be "not a university subject." Geographer Brian Baskerville writes about the end of geography at Harvard.
