Valentine's Day Decorations Kids Can Make

Tuesday January 29, 2013

Here in the Great White Canadian North we have snow days which means my children are home rather than at school where they belong and the weather is usually too wretched to be outside. These days often are spent making some type of delicious treat or decorating cakes and cupcakes together. When I have a big cake project to do that requires Rice Krispie treat constructions the kids get to sculpt using my leftovers. They think this is great fun although their creations often end up a great deal smaller than the original amount of marshmallow mixture warrants!

For Valentine's day we often make heart shaped cookies, chocolate cupcakes adorned with fondant lips and cut out Rice Krispie treats festooned with sprinkles, chocolate and dragees. There is something wonderful watching small hands use a piping bag to draw "I love you" on cookies and even the catastrophic pile of sugar, decorating accents and scraps of fondant are not a chore to clean up. So if the weather is awful, like it is here today, crack open your favorite cookbook, dust off your cake decorating tools and have some messy fun!

Happy Birthday Mac!

Tuesday January 29, 2013

The pictures in my photo albums and on the assorted USB sticks mark off the years in birthday cakes and hand made Halloween costumes. As life gets insanely busy we sometimes don't have the opportunity to snap pictures of everyday events but we always have time to commemorate special kid days. I have made cakes shaped like Transformers, waterfalls, airplanes, Blue's Clues, construction sites, hockey rinks and even the occasional topsy turvey creation over the years with a great deal of love and varying degrees of success.For those who wonder...his cake this year will be an old fashioned galleon caught is a raging wave filled storm. Picking cakes with a great deal of icing (the waves!) has become a well honed skill of my son's over the years! I have enjoyed making every cake and want to say to my oldest baby, Mackenzie who turns 14 this year and is taller than my 5'9" that I hope his birthday is wonderful.

Wide Awake when Picking Ingredients

Tuesday January 29, 2013
Coffee Beans by amanda28192, on Flickr

As a chef I am a coffee junkie who drinks about 6 cups of coffee a day (gasp!) or more. I don't really go in for caffeine energy drinks because I drink coffee for the rich flavor but I can see how some might like the jolt these produce. I was a wee bit taken aback recently when I got the opportunity to sample a birthday cake covered in cute molded fondant animals that hid an interesting ingredient. This cake was made using a so called functional product called Awake Chocolate which a candy bar packed with twice the caffeine found in a serving of Red Bull.

The cake was very nice but I found myself wondering about including ingredients simply for their trendiness or element of originality. I guess the question of creating your cakes with these types of ingredients is entirely up to you and your needs but I think cakes should have integrity as well. Do not use anything that is not of the highest quality or does not fit sublimely with the whole vision of your cake. Some cakes are mean to shock with their decorations or flavor. For example, I have seen disturbing zombie baby cakes complete with blood and entrails as well as tasted a confection that celebrated roasted garlic! That being said, let your creativity flow but don't cave to the pressure of using ingredients that don't add a little something special.

A Taste of Cake

Tuesday January 29, 2013

I am not a reality show junkie although I have to admit to watching the Voice but I am addicted to food related shows so a new show called the Taste was definitely going to draw me in. The premise is kind of like the Voice with blind taste tests by four culinary stars (Anthony Bourdain , Nigella Lawson, Ludo Lefebvre and Brian Malarkey) who then veto or accept the aspiring contestants. I am unapologetic about loving it so far.

I was also delighted to see a home cook, Lauren Scott, present a lovely rich dessert amidst the savory offerings by all the other cooks and chefs. She made what is one of my very favorite cakes, a flourless chocolate cake, which is perfect for a romantic Valentine's Day dessert adorned with simple decorations to be shared with your partner. This cake is lovely with a stenciled dusting of icing sugar or highlighted with a delectable ganache and simple rolled fondant roses. So try your hand at this lovely recipe and create your own cake design to highlight its richness and simplicity.
