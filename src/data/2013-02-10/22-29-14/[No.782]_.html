Oxbow Critical Care Review

Wednesday February 6, 2013

If you've had any pocket pet or herbivorous reptile that has stopped eating you've probably used Critical Care.  This great supplemental food is used for sick, underweight, and anorexic pets and can be syringe fed after mixing with water or juice.  There are a wide variety of animals it can be fed to and is nutritionally complete!  Read more about this product, including some things I don't like, in the new Oxbow Critical Care Review.

Florida Python Challenge 2013

Tuesday February 5, 2013

It's almost over but the "Python Challenge 2013" of Florida has so far killed at least 30 invasive pythons.  This mass, legal, month long, killing spree is meant to tackle the invasive species problem the state is having with Burmese pythons.  Over 1,000 people have signed up to kill these pythons (after taking an online course) but not many snakes have been killed yet.

What are your feelings on this issue?  Do you think the snakes should be killed?  Invasive species are a major problem to ecosystems are do a lot of long term damage.  I'm just not sure that these snakes are being killed humanely.

Invasive Boas in Puerto Rico

Tuesday February 5, 2013

Florida is not alone in the fight against invasive snakes.  Puerto Rico has found an established breeding population of boa constrictors on the island.  Despite the fact that these snakes are illegal to privately own, it is thought that this population originated from the pet trade.  Over 150 snakes have already been found and genetic testing shows they are all closely related, meaning they started from a small population of snakes and bred with each other.

Mass Pet Euthanasia at California Breeder

Tuesday February 5, 2013

Over 19,000 rodents and reptiles were euthanized in Lake Elsinore, California at Global Captive Breeders recently.  These animals were discovered to be living in awful conditions.  Maggot covered bodies and ill-stricken animals were euthanized in the largest animal seizure ever recorded in the state of California.  Over 18,400 rodents and 600 reptiles were euthanized, with over 700 more reptiles and rodents already deceased when the seizure occurred.  No arrests have been made to my knowledge.
