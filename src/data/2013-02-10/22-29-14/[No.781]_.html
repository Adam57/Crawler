New Accounting ETF - FLAG

Tuesday February 5, 2013

Back in October I told you about a new forensic accounting ETF from Exchange traded Concepts (ETC) that was in the works. Well, that fund has finally launched...

  • FLAG - Forensic Accounting ETF

The new ETF correlates with the Del Vecchio Earnings Quality Index, which according to ETC, uses financial statement analysis in an attempt to avoid companies with aggressive revenue recognition while investing in companies that have high earnings quality. FLAG already has about $2.5 million in assets and some of the top holdings include AT&T, Verizon and Texas Instruments.

As of today, the fund is trading around $25.23 and you can get full details for the fund on the ETC FLAG fact sheet

New Corporate Bond ETF - GLCB

Friday February 1, 2013

Yesterday WisdomTree launched a new corporate bond ETF...

  • GLCB - The WisdomTree Global Corporate Bond Fund

The new bond fund is actively managed by Western Asset and invests in corporate fixed income securities from around the world with intermediate-term durations between 2 and 10 years. The fund trades on the Nasdaq and you can get full details on the press release (opens a pdf). Or you can visit the WisdomTree GLCB fact sheet.

New Actively Managed Emerging Markets ETF - Coming Soon

Tuesday January 29, 2013

Emerging Global (EG Shares) has another emerging market ETF in the works, but this particular fund will be actively managed...

  • EGShares Emerging Markets Active ETF

According to the SEC filing, some of the highlights for the fund are...

  • It will invest in leading companies representative of all industries domiciled in emerging market countries
  • It will primarily invest in a non-diversified portfolio of equity securities traded in non-U.S. markets
  • It will invest at least 80% of its net assets in emerging market companies' securities, including small and medium capitalized companies domiciled in emerging market countries having a market capitalization of at least $1 billion at the time of purchase
  • It will invest up to 20% of its assets in other types of securities and investments related to achieving its investment objective
  • It may invest in mortgage- or asset-backed securities
  • It may hold  forward contracts and/or depositary receipts
  • It may at times hold short positions in securities.
