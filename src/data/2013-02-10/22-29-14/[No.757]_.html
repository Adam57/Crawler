Is Sauerkraut the New Viagra?

Saturday February 9, 2013
A Croatian nutritionist claims sauerkraut is the holistic, or natural, version of Viagra. As reported in the Croatian Times in June 2009, in her book "Nutrition of the 21st Century" (published in Croatia in 2009), Lejla Kazinic Kreho says, "Many are completely unaware that pickled cabbage is one of the most-powerful aphrodisiacs."

Pumpkin Sauerkraut Muffins
© 2009 Barbara Rolek licensed to About.com, Inc.


As to its ED solution properties, the debate rages on. It is known, however, that sauerkraut has a raft of health benefits. It's packed with vitamins (especially C) and minerals, is an immune booster, balances the bacteria in your gastrointestinal tract, might be a flu fighter, and its antioxidant properties are thought to combat cancer.

Those facts alone seem like great reasons to try these Eastern European sauerkraut recipes and to take a look at how sauerkraut is made.

Get Ready to Party Hearty on Shrove Tuesday

Friday February 8, 2013

Easter is approaching and, for many, that means fasting for Lent.

Luckily, Mardi Gras, also known as Fat Tuesday or Shrove Tuesday, gives us one last chance to party before Ash Wednesday.

For Polish Americans, Fat Tuesday is Paczki Day. In Lithuania, the day is called Uzgavenes and doughnuts called spurgos are eaten with gusto. Ukranians devour nalysnky, crepe-like pancakes, on Zapusty, their Fat Tuesday.

Get in the spirit of things and delight your family with paczki or Polish funnel cakes before the fasting begins.

Say Cheese with Polish Sweet Rolls

Thursday February 7, 2013

 Polish Cheese Sweet Rolls or Drozdzowki z Serem
© 2011 Barbara Rolek licensed to About.com, Inc.
What better way to say "I love you" than with homemade sweet rolls? Try this easy Polish Cheese Sweet Roll recipe or drozdzówki z serem for brunch or breakfast on St. Valentine's Day. Make the dough up the night before, let them rise for the second time in the morning and have hot sweet rolls on Feb. 14. If sweetened curd cheese isn't your thing, fill them with whatever fruit, poppyseed or nut filling you like.

The word drozdzówki actually translates to "buns" in English and I can remember my mother saying after Sunday morning Mass, "Let's stop at Marek's Bakery for some buns." Danish pastries will forever be known as "buns" in my family and many other Polish families, I'll bet!


Warsaw Bakery Sweet Roll Sign
© 2011 Barbara Rolek licensed to About.com, Inc.
This sweet roll dough is only slightly sweet and not as flaky as a true Danish, but that's the way Poles like them.

Love Apple (Tomato) Recipes Eastern European Style

Thursday February 7, 2013
There's a lot to love about a tomato and Eastern Europeans have been in the know since the seeds arrived from the Americas in the early 16th century. Queen Bona Sforza, Poland's Italian queen, introduced tomatoes to Poland. The Croats' cuisine along the Dalmatian Coast was influenced by their Italian neighbors who were enjoying them while Americans still thought tomatoes were poisonous.


Tomato Feta Tart

It was once believed that tomatoes had aphrodisiacal powers, giving it the moniker "love apple." Whatever you decide to call these luscious fruits (yes, they are fruits, not veggies), try them in these Top Eastern European Tomato Recipes.
